### Building enclosure commissioning (BECx)

### Building enclosure commissioning standard

The project’s building enclosure commissioning (BECx) scope complies with all tasks and deliverables referenced in ASTM E2947-21a, Standard Guide for Building Enclosure Commissioning or equivalent standard (optionally excluding ASTM E2947-21a sections 7.2.4 and 7.4.3). The BECx report is provided in *EAp3: Fundamental Commissioning*.

### Building enclosure commissioning key tasks and milestones

Confirm completion of the following key tasks and milestones in addition to those required by ASTM E2947-21a, and **confirm that associated dates are recorded in the commissioning report**:

- **Building enclosure commissioning provider (BECxP) designation**: Owner designation of the BECxP and initial BECxP involvement occurred during predesign or very early in the design phase, aligned with ASTM E2947-21a intent.
- **QA/QC construction documentation:**The CxP performed QA/QC review of at least 25% of the completed contractors’ checklists and functional tests for commissioned systems.
- **BECxP training materials review:** BECxP review of training materials and confirmation of training has been provided or is identified as an incomplete task in the BECx report.

### Energy Information System (EIS)

### EIS functionality

Confirm that the project has a remotely accessible platform with software functionality to perform smart analytics and visually present metered energy data.

The MBCx plan confirms the following capabilities, as applicable

- Annual energy benchmarking of energy use intensities
- Comparison of all energy consumption to the prior interval annually and monthly
- For electric interval data, hourly loadshape with comparisons
- Comparison of all hourly metered electricity to the prior interval annually, monthly, daily, and hourly
- For tenant spaces > 10,000 ft2 (930 m2), tenant portal with capability to provide visualization and reporting of base building data for shared systems serving the tenants and tenant electricity energy use (excluding shared systems).


 The EIS visually presents all metered data referenced in *EAp4: Energy Metering and Reporting*, plus any large electric equipment referenced above. This includes the following data:

- Small buildings and major renovations. Monthly energy consumption for each energy source, monthly renewable electricity generation (as applicable), and monthly peak electric demand.
- All other projects (subject to ASHRAE 90.1 monitoring requirements). Monthly energy consumption for each non-electric energy source, and hourly data for total electricity, on-site renewable electricity, sub-metered electricity uses, tenant total electricity, tenant sub-metered electricity uses, and large electric chilled water plants, as applicable.

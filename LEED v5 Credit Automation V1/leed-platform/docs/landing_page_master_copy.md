## Facts, Assumptions, And Risks

### Facts

- EcoGen's current product plan is LEED v5-first.
- The production MVP is suite-based, not all-credit automation.
- The first commercial wedge is Water Efficiency.
- Compliance-critical outputs require named human approval.
- V1 should export manual-upload evidence packs; direct Arc or LEED Online submission is V2 or conditional.

### Assumptions

- The launch audience is primarily LEED consultants and sustainability project managers.
- The main conversion goal is demo booking, not self-serve signup.
- The landing page should mirror the reference image's calm, green, evidence-led style.
- Early proof metrics will come from pilots or internal prototypes, not public customer traction yet.

### Risks

- "100% automation" language can create trust, liability, and review-risk problems if used without narrow workflow boundaries.
- SEO support pages can look thin if they are created only for keywords; each needs a concrete checklist, workflow, comparison, or answer.
- LEED v5 requirements and deadlines can change through USGBC/GBCI updates, so source checks need an update cadence.

## Positioning Decision

### Public Category

AI-assisted LEED v5 evidence automation platform.

### One-Liner

EcoGen helps LEED teams turn project data into traceable, reviewer-ready evidence packs for supported LEED v5 credits.

### Sharp Promise

Prepare LEED v5 evidence packs faster, with sources, formulas, confidence flags, and human approvals attached.

### Claim Boundary

Use this language:

- Automates repeatable LEED documentation workflows.
- Prepares reviewer-ready evidence packs.
- Runs deterministic calculations for supported credits.
- Centralizes sources, assumptions, review decisions, and exports.
- Routes compliance-critical outputs through named human approval.

Avoid this language:

- Fully automates LEED certification.
- Guarantees points or certification.
- Replaces LEED consultants.
- Auto-submits to USGBC or Arc in V1.
- Supports all LEED v5 credits equally.

Suggested compromise for the user's "100% automation for some credits" idea:

Selected low-risk workflow steps can run end-to-end once source data is available, but the public claim should remain "automated evidence preparation with human approval." That keeps the story ambitious without creating professional liability or trust problems.

## Brand Voice

The copy should feel like the reference image: calm, architectural, green, precise, and premium. Use short lines, visible proof, and concrete workflow language.

Write like this:

- "Evidence that can survive review."
- "Sources, formulas, and approvals in one place."
- "Automate the repeatable work. Keep experts in control."
- "Know what is draft, in review, submission-ready, submitted, and awarded."

Do not write like this:

- "Next-gen AI for sustainability."
- "Unleash effortless LEED transformation."
- "Guaranteed certification."
- "A chatbot for green buildings."

Marketing brand style:

- Use "EcoGen" in public UI and marketing.
- Use "Ecogen" only where existing legal/internal naming requires it.

### AEO Direct Answer Blocks

Use these concise answer blocks on the page or in FAQ schema.

**What is EcoGen?**
EcoGen is an AI-assisted LEED v5 evidence automation platform for consultants and sustainability teams. It prepares traceable evidence packs for supported credits and routes compliance-critical outputs through human review.

**Does EcoGen replace LEED consultants?**
No. EcoGen automates repetitive evidence preparation, calculations, extraction, and reporting tasks. LEED consultants and qualified reviewers remain responsible for strategy, review, approval, and submission decisions.

**Which LEED v5 credits does EcoGen support first?**
EcoGen's production MVP focuses on suite-based workflows: Water Efficiency, Refrigerant Management, Quality Plans, Integrative Process Assessment, and Low-Emitting Materials. The first commercial wedge is Water Efficiency.

**Can AI fully automate LEED certification?**
Not responsibly. Some evidence workflows can be highly automated, especially deterministic calculations and source-grounded reports, but professional accountability, reviewer judgment, and final submission approval still require qualified humans.

### 15. FAQ Section

**H2:** Questions LEED teams ask before they trust AI.

**Q: Does EcoGen fully automate LEED certification?**
A: No. EcoGen automates repeatable documentation, calculation, extraction, and evidence-pack workflows for supported credits. Consultants and qualified reviewers still make compliance decisions and approve packages before submission.

**Q: What does "reviewer-ready" mean?**
A: A reviewer-ready package includes the credit narrative, source index, calculation trail, evidence matrix, confidence flags, exceptions, and human approval record needed for serious internal review.

**Q: Which credits are supported first?**
A: The first commercial wedge is Water Efficiency, followed by Refrigerant Management, Quality Plans, Integrative Process Assessment, and Low-Emitting Materials as production suite workflows.

**Q: Can EcoGen connect directly to Arc or LEED Online?**
A: V1 should focus on manual-upload evidence exports. Direct Arc or LEED Online submission belongs in V2 after API access, permissions, schema requirements, terms, and final approval flows are verified.

**Q: How does EcoGen handle regional data gaps?**
A: EcoGen should show credit-specific source availability by region. If APIs or public datasets are weak, the workflow switches to assisted or manual mode and surfaces the gap before export.

**Q: Does EcoGen replace specialist reviewers?**
A: No. Specialist reviewers remain responsible for expert-heavy work such as energy model outputs, LCA assumptions, GIS interpretation, material certification exceptions, and project-specific compliance judgment.

**Q: How is AI output checked?**
A: AI-generated content is tied to sources, confidence flags, deterministic calculations where applicable, requirement-to-evidence matrices, and human review tasks.

**Q: Is EcoGen only for LEED v5?**
A: The current product story focuses on LEED v5. Future support for other certification systems should be added only when source requirements, workflows, and review boundaries are clearly defined.

**Q: Who is EcoGen for?**
A: EcoGen is built for LEED consultants, sustainability project managers, specialist reviewers, and owners who need faster documentation without losing evidence integrity.

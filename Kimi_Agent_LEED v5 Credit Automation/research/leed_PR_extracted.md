## 1.3 Intent (Full Text)

> To promote achievement of credits that address geographically sensitive or adaptation-specific environmental, social equity, and public health priorities. To encourage projects to think creatively to test and accelerate new sustainable building practices and strategies.

---

## 1.4 Requirements — Achievement Pathways

PRc1 offers **five distinct pathways** for achieving points. Projects may achieve **any combination** of the following pathways for a **maximum of 9 points**:

### Pathway 1: Regional Priority

**Description:**
Achieve a regional priority credit from USGBC's Project Priority Library. These credits have been identified by USGBC as having additional regional importance for the project's region.

**Key Details:**
- Credits are sourced from the USGBC Project Priority Library
- Credits are geographically/regionally specific
- USGBC determines which credits have "additional regional importance" for each region
- The project must actually **achieve** the identified regional priority credit(s) to earn points under this pathway

**Points Available:** Variable (counts toward the 9-point maximum)

**Data/Documentation Required:**
- Identification of applicable regional priority credits from the USGBC Project Priority Library
- Documentation demonstrating achievement of the identified regional priority credit(s)
- Project location information to confirm regional eligibility

---

### Pathway 2: Project-Type Credits

**Description:**
Achieve a project-type credit from USGBC's Project Priority Library. These credits have been identified by USGBC as addressing unique needs for the given adaptation or building application.

**Key Details:**
- Credits are sourced from the USGBC Project Priority Library
- Credits are specific to project type/adaptation (e.g., Schools, Healthcare, Data Centers, Warehouses, Retail, etc.)
- USGBC identifies which credits address unique needs for each building type
- The project must actually **achieve** the identified project-type credit(s) to earn points

**Points Available:** Variable (counts toward the 9-point maximum)

**Data/Documentation Required:**
- Identification of applicable project-type credits from the USGBC Project Priority Library
- Documentation demonstrating achievement of the identified project-type credit(s)
- Project type classification to confirm eligibility

---

### Pathway 3: Exemplary Performance

**Description:**
Achieve an exemplary performance credit from USGBC's Project Priority Library. These credits have been identified by USGBC as going above and beyond an existing LEED v5 prerequisite or credit in the LEED v5 priority areas of:
- **Scale**
- **Decarbonization**
- **Resilience**
- **Health**
- **Equity**
- **Ecosystems**

**Key Details:**
- Credits must demonstrate performance that exceeds standard LEED v5 requirements
- Exemplary performance must be in one or more of the six priority areas listed above
- Credits are sourced from the USGBC Project Priority Library
- The project must achieve performance that goes significantly beyond the base prerequisite or credit requirements

**Points Available:** Variable (counts toward the 9-point maximum)

**Data/Documentation Required:**
- Identification of applicable exemplary performance credits from the USGBC Project Priority Library
- Quantified documentation demonstrating performance that exceeds the standard requirement threshold
- Documentation must clearly show the baseline requirement vs. the achieved performance level

---

### Pathway 4: Pilot Credits

**Description:**
Achieve a pilot credit from USGBC's Project Priority Library.

**Key Details:**
- Pilot credits are new credits being tested by USGBC for potential inclusion in future versions of the rating system
- Credits are sourced from the USGBC Project Priority Library
- These credits allow projects to test and accelerate new sustainable building practices
- Pilot credits may address strategies not currently covered by the standard LEED v5 rating system

**Points Available:** Variable (counts toward the 9-point maximum)

**Data/Documentation Required:**
- Identification of applicable pilot credits from the USGBC Project Priority Library
- Full documentation demonstrating compliance with the pilot credit requirements
- Any special reporting or feedback documentation that USGBC requires for pilot credit participation

---

### Pathway 5: Innovative Strategies

**Description:**
Achieve significant, measurable, environmental performance using a strategy **not addressed** in the LEED v5 green building rating system.

**Key Details:**
- The innovation strategy must NOT already be addressed in LEED v5
- Must demonstrate **significant, measurable environmental performance**
- Projects must identify **all four of the following criteria**:
  1. **The intent** of the proposed innovation strategy
  2. **Proposed requirements** for compliance
  3. **Proposed submittals** to demonstrate compliance
  4. **The design approach or strategies** used to meet the requirements

**Points Available:** Variable (counts toward the 9-point maximum)

**Data/Documentation Required:**
- Written narrative describing the intent of the proposed innovation strategy
- Clearly defined proposed requirements for compliance (i.e., what must be done to achieve the innovation)
- Clearly defined proposed submittals to demonstrate compliance (i.e., what documentation will be provided to USGBC/GBCI)
- Detailed description of the design approach or strategies used to meet the requirements
- Quantified data demonstrating significant, measurable environmental performance

## 1.6 Documentation Requirements for USGBC/GBCI Submission

### For Regional Priority Pathway:
- [ ] Printout or reference showing the credit(s) identified as regional priorities for the project's specific region from the USGBC Project Priority Library
- [ ] All standard documentation required for the underlying credit being claimed as a regional priority
- [ ] Evidence that the project is located within the applicable region

### For Project-Type Credits Pathway:
- [ ] Printout or reference showing the credit(s) identified as project-type credits for the project's building type from the USGBC Project Priority Library
- [ ] All standard documentation required for the underlying project-type credit(s)
- [ ] Evidence confirming the project's building type classification

### For Exemplary Performance Pathway:
- [ ] Printout or reference showing the credit(s) eligible for exemplary performance from the USGBC Project Priority Library
- [ ] Documentation demonstrating that the achieved performance exceeds the standard requirement
- [ ] Quantified performance data showing the extent of exceedance
- [ ] Narrative explaining how the exemplary performance addresses the priority areas (scale, decarbonization, resilience, health, equity, ecosystems)

### For Pilot Credits Pathway:
- [ ] Identification of the pilot credit(s) from the USGBC Project Priority Library
- [ ] All documentation required by the specific pilot credit(s)
- [ ] Any pilot credit-specific reporting forms or feedback documentation required by USGBC

### For Innovative Strategies Pathway:
- [ ] **Innovation Proposal Document** containing all four required criteria:
  1. **Intent statement**: Clearly state the intent of the proposed innovation strategy
  2. **Proposed requirements**: Define what must be done to comply with the innovation
  3. **Proposed submittals**: Define what documentation will be submitted to demonstrate compliance
  4. **Design approach**: Describe the design approach or strategies used to meet the requirements
- [ ] Quantified performance data demonstrating significant, measurable environmental performance
- [ ] Evidence that the strategy is **not already addressed** in the LEED v5 rating system
- [ ] Calculations, measurements, or modeling results supporting the claimed environmental performance

## 1.10 Consultant Production Checklist — PRc1

### Tasks the consultant must produce:

- [ ] **Step 1**: Access and review the USGBC Project Priority Library to identify available credits for the project's region and building type
- [ ] **Step 2**: Coordinate with the project team to determine which pathways (Regional Priority, Project-Type Credits, Exemplary Performance, Pilot Credits, Innovative Strategies) will be pursued

- [ ] **Step 4**: For Project-Type Credits pathway — confirm building type classification and match to available project-type credits; document achievement
- [ ] **Step 5**: For Exemplary Performance pathway — identify credits where the project significantly exceeds standard requirements; prepare quantitative documentation showing exceedance

- [ ] **Step 7**: For Innovative Strategies pathway — develop Innovation Proposal Document with all four required criteria (intent, proposed requirements, proposed submittals, design approach); prepare quantitative performance documentation
- [ ] **Step 8**: Compile all documentation into the LEED submission package
- [ ] **Step 9**: Track points from all pathways to ensure maximum of 9 points is not exceeded (or to optimize for the full 9 points)

## 2.3 Intent (Full Text)

> To encourage team integration required by a LEED AP and to streamline the application and certification process.

---

## 2.4 Requirements — Achievement Pathway

### Single Pathway: LEED AP on Project Team

**Requirement Statement:**
At least one principal participant of the project team must be a **LEED AP with a specialty appropriate for the project**.

**Key Details:**
- The LEED AP must be a **principal participant** of the project team
- The LEED AP must have a **specialty appropriate for the project**
- **Only one (1) point** is available for this credit — there is no multi-point scale
- The credit applies equally to both New Construction and Core and Shell projects

### Eligible LEED AP Specialties

The following LEED AP specialties are relevant for BD+C projects:

| LEED AP Specialty | Appropriate For |
|-------------------|-----------------|
| LEED AP BD+C (Building Design + Construction) | Directly appropriate for all BD+C projects |
| LEED AP O+M (Operations + Maintenance) | Generally not appropriate for new construction |
| LEED AP ID+C (Interior Design + Construction) | May be appropriate for certain Core and Shell tenant coordination aspects |
| LEED AP ND (Neighborhood Development) | May be appropriate for projects within neighborhood developments |
| LEED AP Homes | Appropriate for residential BD+C projects |

**Note:** The standard expectation is that a **LEED AP BD+C** specialty would be the most directly appropriate credential for LEED v5 BD+C projects.

### "Principal Participant" Definition

The requirement specifies that the LEED AP must be a "principal participant." This means:
- The individual must hold a key role on the project team
- The individual must be actively involved in the project from an early stage
- The individual should be listed as a core team member in the LEED application
- Typical qualifying roles include: Project Manager, Sustainability Consultant, Architect, Engineer, or other key design/consulting team members

---

## 2.5 Points Available

| Rating System | Points |
|---------------|--------|
| New Construction | 1 point |
| Core and Shell | 1 point |
| **Total Possible** | **1 point** (no variation or exemplary performance) |

---

## 2.6 Documentation Requirements for USGBC/GBCI Submission

### Required Documentation:

1. **LEED AP Credential Verification**
   - [ ] LEED AP credential number of the qualifying team member
   - [ ] Name of the LEED AP as it appears on their credential
   - [ ] Specialty designation (e.g., BD+C, O+M, ID+C, ND, Homes)
   - [ ] Credential must be **active** (not expired or revoked) at the time of project registration or submission

2. **Project Team Role Documentation**
   - [ ] Confirmation that the LEED AP is a **principal participant** on the project team
   - [ ] Description of the LEED AP's role and responsibilities on the project
   - [ ] Evidence of active participation throughout the project (not just a figurehead)

3. **LEED Online Entry**
   - [ ] The LEED AP must be listed in the appropriate field in the LEED Online project registration or team page
   - [ ] GBCI verifies credential validity through the USGBC credential database

### Verification Process:
- GBCI/USGBC will verify the LEED AP credential through their credential registry
- The credential must be current and active
- The specialty must be appropriate for the project type

---

## 2.7 Calculations Required

**None.** This credit requires no mathematical calculations. It is a binary achievement credit:
- Qualifying LEED AP on the project team = 1 point
- No qualifying LEED AP on the project team = 0 points

# MRp1: PLANNING FOR ZERO WASTE OPERATIONS

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Credit ID** | MRp1 |
| **Type** | Prerequisite (Required) |
| **New Construction** | Required |
| **Core and Shell** | Required |
| **Impact Area** | Ecological Conservation and Restoration |
| **Points** | N/A |

## Intent

To reduce the amount of waste that is generated by building occupants and hauled to and disposed of in landfills and incinerators through reduction, reuse, and recycling services and education, and to conserve natural resources for future generations. To set the building up for success in pursuing zero waste operations.

## Requirements

### Achievement Pathways

**New Construction and Core and Shell:**

Must comply with BOTH of the following:

#### 1. Storage and Collection of Recyclables

Provide dedicated areas accessible to waste haulers, janitorial staff, and building occupants for the collection and storage of recyclable materials for the entire building.

**Specific Requirements:**
- Collection and storage areas may be separate locations.
- Recyclable materials **must include:**
  - Organics/food waste
  - Mixed paper
  - Corrugated cardboard
  - Glass
  - Plastics
  - Metals
  - Mixed recyclables are acceptable for paper, corrugated cardboard, glass, plastics, and metals if required by local conditions.
  - Space for the storage of organics/food waste recycling is required **even if service is not available at the time of building occupancy.**
- Take appropriate measures for the safe collection, storage, and disposal of:
  - Batteries
  - Mercury-containing lamps
  - Electronic waste

#### 2. Zero Waste Operations Planning

Include design details, maintenance manuals, and/or other resources from the design and construction team that help facilitate building occupants and operators to meet high-performance waste prevention and recycling goals once in operation.

**Core and Shell only:** Communicate the building's infrastructure and service options information in the tenant guidelines.

## Documentation Required

1. Site plans showing dedicated collection and storage areas for recyclables
2. Documentation that recyclable material types are addressed (organics, paper, cardboard, glass, plastics, metals)
3. Documentation of safe handling measures for batteries, mercury-containing lamps, and e-waste
4. Zero waste operations plan (design details, maintenance manuals, resources)
5. **Core and Shell:** Evidence that waste infrastructure information is included in tenant guidelines

## Data to Submit to USGBC

- Narrative or plan describing waste prevention and recycling strategies
- Documentation of recycling infrastructure and storage areas

## Referenced Standards

- None specifically cited (prerequisite relies on project-specific planning)

# MRp2: QUANTIFY AND ASSESS EMBODIED CARBON

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Credit ID** | MRp2 |
| **Type** | Prerequisite (Required) |
| **New Construction** | Required |
| **Core and Shell** | Required |
| **Impact Area** | Decarbonization |
| **Points** | N/A |

## Intent

To quantify the embodied carbon impacts of the structure, enclosure, and hardscape of a project and assess the top sources of embodied carbon.

## Requirements

### Achievement Pathways

**New Construction and Core and Shell:**

Must comply with BOTH of the following:

#### 1. Embodied Carbon Quantification

- Quantify the embodied carbon impacts -- **global warming potential (GWP)** -- of the **structure, enclosure, and hardscape materials** for the project.
- **All ancillary structures**, such as parking structures or outbuildings within the LEED project boundary, **must be included** in the calculations.
- **At a minimum, include:**
  - Asphalt
  - Concrete
  - Masonry
  - Structural steel
  - Insulation
  - Aluminum extrusions
  - Structural wood and composites
  - Cladding
  - Glass
- Quantify the **cradle-to-gate (A1-A3)** embodied carbon emissions for each material, defined as the product's **GWP/unit times the amount of material used**.
  - Alternatively, projects using life-cycle assessment or embodied carbon software tools may report A1-A3 results from their tool.

#### 2. High-Priority Embodied Carbon Sources

- Identify the **top three sources of embodied carbon** on the project.
- Describe how **project-specific strategies** were considered to reduce the impacts of these hot spots.

**Core and Shell only:** Communicate the embodied carbon measurements and material suppliers in the tenant guidelines.

## Documentation Required

1. Embodied carbon quantification report covering structure, enclosure, and hardscape
2. GWP/unit data for all specified materials (A1-A3)
3. Material quantity calculations
4. Identification of top 3 embodied carbon sources
5. Narrative describing strategies considered to reduce hot spot impacts
6. **Core and Shell:** Evidence that embodied carbon data is included in tenant guidelines

## Data to Submit to USGBC

- Embodied carbon quantification (GWP values for all materials in scope)
- Material quantities and GWP/unit calculations
- Identification of top 3 sources and reduction strategies narrative

## Calculations Required

- **Cradle-to-Gate (A1-A3) GWP** = Sum of (GWP/unit for each material x quantity of each material)
- Must include all materials listed above at minimum
- All ancillary structures within LEED project boundary must be included

## Referenced Standards

- **ISO 21930** (implied for LCA methodology)
- **EN 15804** (implied for EPD/LCA methodology)
- Cradle-to-gate (A1-A3) boundary as defined by ISO 21930/EN 15804

# MRc1: BUILDING AND MATERIALS REUSE

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Credit ID** | MRc1 |
| **Type** | Credit |
| **New Construction** | 1-3 points |
| **Core and Shell** | 1-5 points |
| **Impact Area** | Decarbonization, Ecological Conservation and Restoration |

## Intent

To incorporate reused materials into new building design, thereby reducing embodied carbon, keeping materials in circularity, reducing demand for virgin material sourcing, preserving resources and histories, and increasing demand for reused materials.

## Requirements

### Achievement Pathways

**New Construction:**
- Option 1. Building Reuse AND/OR
- Option 2. Materials Reuse

**Core and Shell:**
- Option 1. Building Reuse AND/OR
- Option 2. Materials Reuse

---

### Option 1: Building Reuse

**(1-3 points New Construction, 1-5 points Core and Shell)**

Maintain the existing building structure, including floor decking, roof decking, and enclosure. Calculate reuse of the existing project area according to the tables below.

**Exclusion:** Portions of buildings deemed structurally unsound or hazardous are excluded from the credit calculations.

#### Table 1: Points for Reuse of Existing Building Structure and Enclosure Elements - New Construction

| Percentage of Existing Structure and Enclosure Reuse by Project Area | Points |
|----------------------------------------------------------------------|--------|
| 20% | 1 |
| 35% | 2 |
| 50% | 3 |

#### Table 2: Points for Reuse of Existing Building Structure and Enclosure Elements - Core and Shell

| Percentage of Existing Structure and Enclosure Reuse by Project Area | Points |
|----------------------------------------------------------------------|--------|
| 10% | 1 |
| 20% | 2 |
| 30% | 3 |
| 40% | 4 |
| 50% | 5 |

---

### Option 2: Materials Reuse

**(1-2 points)**

- Survey and identify opportunities for materials reuse and/or procurement of reused materials from off-site.
- Reuse materials by keeping them in place or acquiring them from applicable salvage sources or reuse markets and incorporating the materials into the new project design.

**High-Value Targeted Materials:**
Specific targeted materials are valued higher because they have:
- High impacts (embodied carbon or pollution)
- Are hard to recycle
- Significant amounts end up in landfill

**Deconstruction Projects:**
- For projects with deconstruction or demolition in scope, conduct a **salvage assessment** prior to deconstruction or demolition activities.
- Identify materials that can be retained on-site or diverted off-site to reuse markets.
- Salvaged materials sent for **off-site reuse** contribute to **MRc5: Construction and Demolition Waste Diversion**.
- Materials **retained on-site** contribute to this credit option.

#### Calculation: Equation 1 - Reuse % per material type

```
Reuse % per material type = Amount of material type reused / Total amount of material type in New Construction scope
```

#### Table 3: Points for Incorporating Reused Materials

| Reuse Materials Threshold | Points |
|---------------------------|--------|
| Reuse at least 15% of 1 targeted material type | 1 point |
| OR Reuse at least 15% of 2 other material types | 1 point |
| OR Reuse an equivalent weighted average of targeted and other material types | 1 point |

| Reuse Materials Threshold | Points |
|---------------------------|--------|
| Reuse at least 30% of 1 targeted material type | 2 points |
| OR Reuse at least 15% of 2 targeted material types | 2 points |
| OR Reuse at least 15% of 4 other material types | 2 points |
| OR Reuse an equivalent weighted average of targeted and other material types | 2 points |

#### Table 4: Reuse Material Types and Correlating Units

**Targeted Materials (valued higher):**

| Material Type | Unit |
|--------------|------|
| Carpeting | Surface area |
| Ceilings | Surface area |
| Furniture (ancillary and systems) | Pieces, weight, volume, or floor area |
| Interior walls | Linear or surface area |

**Other Materials:**

| Material Type | Unit |
|--------------|------|
| Dimensional lumber | Board foot or linear |
| Doors | Count |
| Casework | Linear |
| Floor-covering materials (not including carpet) | Surface area |
| Lighting fixtures | Count |
| Plumbing fixtures | Count |
| Mechanical equipment | Count |
| Door hardware | Count |
| Project defined other | Project defined |

## Documentation Required

1. **Building Reuse:** Calculations showing percentage of existing structure and enclosure reused
2. **Materials Reuse:** Salvage assessment report (if deconstruction is in scope)
3. Documentation of reused materials, sources, and quantities
4. Calculations per Equation 1 for each material type
5. Evidence of incorporation into new project design

## Data to Submit to USGBC

- Reuse percentage calculations
- Material quantities (reused vs. total for each type)
- Source documentation for salvaged materials
- Salvage assessment (if applicable)

## Calculations Required

- **Building Reuse %** = Area of existing structure/enclosure reused / Total project area
- **Material Reuse %** (per Equation 1) = Amount of material type reused / Total amount of material type in NC scope
- Weighted average calculations across material types

## Referenced Standards

- None specifically cited

# MRc2: REDUCE EMBODIED CARBON

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Credit ID** | MRc2 |
| **Type** | Credit |
| **New Construction** | 1-6 points (20% reduction required for Platinum) |
| **Core and Shell** | 1-8 points (20% reduction required for Platinum) |
| **Impact Area** | Decarbonization |

## Intent

To track and reduce embodied carbon of major structural, enclosure, and hardscape materials from construction processes on new construction and renovation projects.

## Requirements

### Achievement Pathways

**New Construction:**
- Option 1. Whole-Building Life-Cycle Assessment AND/OR
- Option 2. Environmental Product Declaration (EPD) Analysis (Path 1 or Path 2) AND/OR
- Option 3. Track Carbon Emissions from Construction Activities

**Core and Shell:**
- Option 1. Whole-Building Life-Cycle Assessment AND/OR
- Option 2. Environmental Product Declaration (EPD) Analysis (Path 1 or Path 2) AND/OR
- Option 3. Track Carbon Emissions from Construction Activities (Path 1 or Path 2)

**General Requirements for All Options:**
- Quantify the reduction of embodied carbon of major **structure, enclosure, and hardscape materials**.
- **All ancillary structures**, such as parking structures or outbuildings within the LEED project boundary, **must be included** in the calculations.
- Both baseline projects and final results may use as-designed or as-constructed final quantities, provided that quantities did not change more than 10% from design through construction.
- Results must be based on embodied carbon intensities of materials as constructed.
- Projects may earn up to 6 points total (NC) or up to 8 points total (Core and Shell).

---

### Points Tables

#### Table 1: Points for Embodied Carbon Reductions - New Construction

| Reduction Level | Option 1: WBLCA | Option 2 Path 1: Project-Average | Option 2 Path 2: Materials-Type |
|-----------------|-----------------|----------------------------------|---------------------------------|
| Meet baseline or industry average | 2 points | 1 point | Three material categories for 1 point |
| 10% reduction in GWP | 3 points | -- | -- |
| 20% reduction in GWP | 4 points | 2 points | -- |
| 30% reduction in GWP | 5 points | -- | Five or more material categories for 2 points |
| 40%+ reduction in GWP | 6 points | 3 points | -- |

**Note:** Meeting the baseline or industry average in Table 1 can achieve no more than 2 points.

#### Table 2: Points for Embodied Carbon Reductions - Core and Shell

| Reduction Level | Option 1: WBLCA | Option 2 Path 1: Project-Average | Option 2 Path 2: Materials-Type |
|-----------------|-----------------|----------------------------------|---------------------------------|
| Meet baseline or industry average | 2 points | 1 point | Two material categories for 1 point |
| 10% reduction in GWP | 3 points | -- | -- |
| 20% reduction in GWP | 4 points | 2 points | -- |
| 30% reduction in GWP | 5 points | -- | Four material categories for 2 points |
| 40%+ reduction in GWP | 6 points | 3 points | -- |
| 50%+ reduction in GWP | 7 points | 4 points | Six or more material categories for 3 points |

**Note:** Meeting the baseline or industry average in Table 2 can achieve no more than 2 points.

---

### Option 1: Whole-Building Life-Cycle Assessment

**(1-6 points NC, 1-7 points Core and Shell)**

- Conduct a **cradle-to-grave (modules A-C, excluding operating energy and operating water-related energy)** whole-building life-cycle assessment (WBLCA) of the project's **structure, enclosure, and hardscape materials**.
- Compare results to a **baseline** developed for the project.
- Earn points according to Table 1 (NC) or Table 2 (Core and Shell).

**Required Impact Categories in WBLCA Report:**

| Impact Category | Unit |
|----------------|------|
| GWP (greenhouse gases) | kg CO2e |
| Depletion of the stratospheric ozone layer | kg CFC-11e |
| Acidification of land and water sources | moles H+ or kg SO2e |
| Eutrophication | kg nitrogen equivalent or kg phosphate equivalent |
| Formation of tropospheric ozone | kg NOx, kg O3e, or kg ethene |
| Depletion of nonrenewable energy resources | megajoules (MJ) using CML/depletion of fossil fuels in TRACI |

---

### Option 2: Environmental Product Declaration (EPD) Analysis

**(1-3 points NC, 1-4 points Core and Shell)**

#### Path 1: Project-Average Approach

- Earn points for reducing embodied carbon of the project based on **EPD data** for the procured materials compared to **industry average values**.
- Points are awarded according to Table 1 for the **whole-project weighted average** of applicable material categories.
- **Industry averages** for material categories are defined by:
  - U.S. EPA
  - Most recent Carbon Leadership Forum (CLF) Material Baselines report
  - Similarly robust and widely recognized publications
  - Industry-wide EPDs applicable to the project region
- Projects must **track the GWP/unit** of the materials installed and reconcile the design-phase embodied carbon intensities if materials or GWP values have changed.
- The reconciliation of material quantities is not necessary unless quantities have changed more than 10% from design through construction.
- Projects must use **project-specific material quantities** and identify **product-specific or facility-specific Type III EPDs** for covered materials to demonstrate reductions.
- **Biogenic carbon** may only be included for calculations that include C-stage emissions.

#### Path 2: Materials-Type Approach

**(1-2 points NC, 1-3 points Core and Shell)**

- Earn points by demonstrating that **structural, enclosure, and hardscape materials** for targeted material types have lower embodied carbon impacts than **industry benchmarks** as demonstrated by **product-specific Type III EPDs**.
- Track the **GWP per unit** of the materials installed, reconciling the design-phase embodied carbon intensities if materials or GWP values have changed.
- The reconciliation of material quantities is not necessary unless quantities have changed more than 10% from design through construction.
- A **weighted average approach** can be used to calculate average embodied carbon intensity values within a product category.
- **Industry averages** for embodied carbon intensity values are defined by:
  - U.S. EPA
  - Most recent CLF Material Baselines report
  - Similarly robust and widely recognized publications
  - Industry-wide EPDs applicable to the project region

---

### Option 3: Track Carbon Emissions from Construction Activities

**(1-2 points)**

Earn points for tracking carbon emissions during construction activities according to Table 3.

#### Table 3: Points for Tracking Emissions During Construction Activities

| Pathway | Type of Construction-phase Emissions to Track | LCA Modules | Points |
|---------|-----------------------------------------------|-------------|--------|
| Path 1 | Track all fuel and utility usage for contractor jobsite operations | A5 | 1 |
| Path 2 | Track all fuel and utility usage for contractor and subcontractor jobsite operations | A5 | 2 |

## Documentation Required

1. **Option 1 (WBLCA):** Complete LCA report with all required impact categories, baseline vs. proposed comparison
2. **Option 2 (EPD Analysis):** EPD documentation for all covered materials, GWP/unit tracking, industry average benchmarks
3. **Option 3 (Construction Activities):** Fuel and utility tracking logs for construction operations
4. Material quantity reconciliations (if quantities changed >10% from design to construction)
5. Evidence of material as-constructed embodied carbon intensities

## Data to Submit to USGBC

- WBLCA report (Option 1) or EPD analysis (Option 2) or construction emissions tracking (Option 3)
- GWP values for all materials (kg CO2e)
- Material quantities
- Baseline comparison documentation
- All six mandatory impact category results (for Option 1)

## Calculations Required

### Option 1 (WBLCA):
- Baseline building GWP (kg CO2e)
- Proposed building GWP (kg CO2e)
- Percentage reduction = (Baseline - Proposed) / Baseline x 100

### Option 2 (EPD Analysis):
- Project-average GWP/unit = Sum(Product GWP/unit x Quantity) / Total Quantity
- Comparison against industry average GWP/unit
- Materials-type weighted average calculations

### Option 3 (Construction Activities):
- Total fuel and utility usage during construction (A5 emissions)
- Conversion to CO2e equivalent

## Referenced Standards

- **ISO 21930** - Sustainability in buildings and civil engineering works -- Core rules for environmental product declarations of construction products and services
- **EN 15804** - Sustainability of construction works -- Environmental product declarations -- Core rules for the product category of construction products
- **ISO 14040/14044** - Life cycle assessment principles and framework
- **TRACI** - Tool for the Reduction and Assessment of Chemical and Other Environmental Impacts
- **CML** - Centre for Environmental Studies methodology
- **U.S. EPA** benchmarks
- **Carbon Leadership Forum (CLF) Material Baselines** report
- **Type III EPDs** per ISO 14025 / EN 15804

# MRc3: LOW-EMITTING MATERIALS

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Credit ID** | MRc3 |
| **Type** | Credit |
| **New Construction** | 1-2 points |
| **Core and Shell** | 1 point |
| **Impact Area** | Quality of Life |

## Intent

To reduce concentrations of chemical contaminants that can damage air quality and the environment. To protect human health and the comfort of installers and building occupants.

## Requirements

### Achievement Pathways

**New Construction:** Specify and install permanently installed products, paints, coatings, adhesives, sealants, flooring, walls, ceilings, insulation, furniture, and/or composite wood products that meet the low-emitting criteria.

**Core and Shell:** Same as New Construction.

---

### Table 1: Thresholds for Low-Emitting Materials - New Construction

| Pathway | Product Categories | Threshold | Points |
|---------|-------------------|-----------|--------|
| Path 1 | Achieve **all three** categories: Paints and coatings, Flooring, Ceilings | >90% of all products in each product category | 1 |
| Path 2 | Achieve Path 1, plus **any two** of these additional categories: Adhesives and sealants, Walls, Insulation, Composite wood | >80% of each additional product category | 2 |
| Path 3 | Achieve Path 1 plus the **furniture** category | >80% of the furniture product category | 2 |

### Table 2: Thresholds for Low-Emitting Materials - Core and Shell

| Product Categories | Threshold | Points |
|-------------------|-----------|--------|
| Achieve **any three** categories: Paints and coatings, Flooring, Ceilings, Adhesives and sealants, Walls, Insulation, Composite wood | >90% of all products in each product category | 1 |

**Core and Shell only:** Products to be specified and installed by tenants may be excluded. Communicate the base building's product list in the tenant guidelines.

---

### Product Categories - Detailed Definitions

**Excluded products** (not applicable to low-emitting materials categories):
- Equipment related to fire suppression, HVAC (including ductwork), plumbing, electrical, conveying and communications systems
- Poured concrete
- Structural framing
- Structural insulated panels (SIPs)
- Water-resistive barriers (material installed on a substrate to prevent bulk water intrusion)

#### Paints and Coatings
- By **volume, cost, or surface area**, must meet VOC emissions evaluation criteria
- Includes all interior paints and coatings wet-applied on-site
- **Exclude:** foamed-in-place and sprayed insulation (include in insulation category)

#### Adhesives and Sealants
- By **volume or cost**, must meet VOC emissions evaluation criteria
- Includes all interior adhesives and sealants wet-applied on-site, including those used to install air or vapor barrier membranes and floor-setting materials

#### Flooring
- By **surface area or cost**, must meet VOC emissions evaluation criteria
- Includes all types of hard and soft surface flooring finishes (carpet, ceramic tile, vinyl, rubber, engineered wood, solid wood, stone, laminate), raised flooring systems, entryway ("walk-off") systems, area rugs, wood subflooring, underlayments, sandwich panels, and air barrier membranes and vapor barrier/vapor retarder membranes (if used inside an air barrier membrane)
- **Exclude:** poured concrete, composite wood subflooring (include in composite wood category), wet-applied products applied on the floor

#### Walls
- By **surface area or cost**, must meet VOC emissions evaluation criteria
- Includes all finish wall treatments (wall coverings or wall tile), finish carpentry (millwork, paneling, railings, trim/moldings), gypsum wallboard, wall base/skirting, interior and exterior doors, nonstructural wall framing, and nonstructural sandwich panels
- **Exclude:** wet-applied products applied on the wall, case goods, cabinetry (furniture category), countertops (furniture category), bathroom accessories, door hardware, curtain wall and storefront systems

#### Ceilings
- By **surface area or cost**, must meet VOC emissions evaluation criteria
- Includes all types of ceiling finishes (ceiling panels and ceiling tile), suspension grids, surface ceiling structures (gypsum wallboard or plaster), suspended systems (including canopies and clouds), and nonstructural sandwich panels
- **Exclude:** wet-applied products applied on the ceiling and corrugated metal decking

#### Insulation
- By **surface area or cost**, must meet VOC emissions evaluation criteria
- Includes all thermal and acoustic boards, batts (faced and unfaced), rolls, blankets, sound attenuation fire blankets, and foamed-in-place, loose-fill, blown, and sprayed insulation
- **Exclude:** insulation installed outside an air barrier membrane

#### Furniture
- By **cost, area, or number of units**, must meet furniture emissions evaluation criteria or VOC emissions evaluation criteria
- Includes all permanently installed office furniture, cubicles/systems furniture, seating, desks, tables, filing/storage, specialty items, beds, case goods, casework, countertops, movable/demountable partitions, bathroom/toilet partitions, shelving, lockers, retail fixtures (including slatwall), window treatments, and furnishing items (nonfixed area rugs, cubicle curtains, mattresses)
- A custom item is compliant if all components are declared under furniture category and meet VOC emissions evaluation criteria, OR if the finished piece meets furniture emissions evaluation or VOC emissions evaluation criteria
- **Exclude:** office and bathroom accessories, art, recreational items, cabinet and drawer hardware, planters

#### Composite Wood
- By **surface area or cost**, must meet formaldehyde emissions evaluation criteria
- Includes all particleboard, medium density fiberboard (medium density and thin), hardwood plywood with veneer, composite or combination core, and wood structural panels or structural wood products

---

### Low-Emitting Criteria - Detailed Standards

#### VOC Emissions Evaluation Criteria

Products must meet ONE of the following:

1. **Third-party certification:** Product has a qualifying third-party certification, valid at the time of product purchase, that demonstrates testing and compliance according to the **California Department of Public Health (CDPH) Standard Method v1.2-2017** using the **private office scenario**. Products used in classrooms may be modeled using the schools or private office scenario.

2. **Qualified independent laboratory report:** Product has a qualifying laboratory report (or summary) demonstrating the product has been tested no more than three years prior to the product's purchase, according to the **CDPH Standard Method v1.2-2017**. Products must meet the VOC limits in **Table 4-1 of the private office scenario**. Products used in classrooms may be modeled using the schools or private office scenario.

3. **Product is inherently non-emitting, salvaged, or reused.**

#### Furniture Emissions Evaluation Criteria

Products must meet ONE of the following:

1. **Third-party certification:** Product has a qualifying third-party certification, valid at the time of product purchase, that demonstrates testing according to **ANSI/BIFMA Standard Method M7.1-2011 (R2021)** and complies with specific sections of the **ANSI/BIFMA e3-2014 or e3-2024, "Furniture Sustainability Standard."** Statements of product compliance must include the exposure scenario(s).
   - Seating products must be evaluated using the **seating scenario**
   - Classroom furniture must be evaluated using the **standard school classroom scenario**
   - Other products should be evaluated using the **open plan** or **private office scenario** (open plan is more stringent)

2. **Product is inherently nonemitting, salvaged, or reused.**

#### Formaldehyde Emissions Evaluation Criteria

Product has a qualifying third-party certification from a **California Air Resources Board (CARB)-approved/EPA-recognized third-party certifier (TPC)**, valid at the time of product purchase, demonstrating the product is one of the following:

1. Certified as **ultra-low-emitting formaldehyde (ULEF)** product under the EPA Toxic Substances Control Act, Formaldehyde Emission Standards for Composite Wood Products (**TSCA, Title VI**) or **CARB Airborne Toxic Control Measure (ATCM)**
2. Certified as **no added formaldehyde resins (NAF)** product under EPA TSCA Title VI or CARB ATCM
3. **Wood structural panels** manufactured according to **PS 1-09 or PS 2-10** (or equivalent) and labeled bond classification **Exposure 1 or Exterior**
4. **Structural wood product** manufactured according to:
   - **ASTM D5456** (for structural composite lumber)
   - **ANSI A190.1** (for glued laminated timber)
   - **ASTM D5055** (for I-joists)
   - **ANSI PRG 320** (for cross-laminated timber)
   - **PS 20-15** (for finger-jointed lumber)
5. Product is inherently non-emitting, salvaged, or reused.

## Documentation Required

1. Product list showing all products in each category
2. Third-party certifications or laboratory test reports for each product
3. Percentage calculations for each product category
4. Furniture emissions documentation (if applicable)
5. Composite wood formaldehyde compliance documentation
6. **Core and Shell:** Evidence that base building product list is included in tenant guidelines

## Data to Submit to USGBC

- Product category compliance documentation
- Third-party certifications or lab reports
- Percentage compliance calculations for each category
- Product list with manufacturer information

## Referenced Standards

- **CDPH Standard Method v1.2-2017** - California Department of Public Health Standard Method for the Testing and Evaluation of Volatile Organic Chemical Emissions from Indoor Sources Using Environmental Chambers
- **ANSI/BIFMA Standard Method M7.1-2011 (R2021)** - Standard Test Method for Determining VOC Emissions from Office Furniture Systems, Components and Seating
- **ANSI/BIFMA e3-2014 or e3-2024** - Furniture Sustainability Standard
- **EPA TSCA Title VI** - Formaldehyde Emission Standards for Composite Wood Products
- **CARB ATCM** - California Air Resources Board Airborne Toxic Control Measure
- **ASTM D5456** - Standard Specification for Evaluation of Structural Composite Lumber Products
- **ANSI A190.1** - Standard for Glued Laminated Timber
- **ASTM D5055** - Standard Specification for Establishing and Monitoring Structural Capacities of Prefabricated Wood I-Joists
- **ANSI PRG 320** - Standard for Performance-Rated Cross-Laminated Timber
- **PS 1-09 / PS 2-10** - Voluntary Product Standards for Structural Plywood / Wood-Based Structural-Use Panels
- **PS 20-15** - American Softwood Lumber Standard

# MRc4: BUILDING PRODUCT SELECTION AND PROCUREMENT

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Credit ID** | MRc4 |
| **Type** | Credit |
| **New Construction** | 1-5 points |
| **Core and Shell** | 1-5 points |
| **Impact Area** | Decarbonization, Quality of Life, Ecological Conservation and Restoration |

## Intent

To encourage the use of products and materials that have sustainability information available and that have environmentally, economically, and socially preferable impacts in alignment with industry momentum. To reward project teams for selecting products from manufacturers who have disclosed sustainability information about their products and have optimized their products across multiple criteria areas.

## Requirements

### Achievement Pathways

**New Construction and Core and Shell:**

Select nonstructural building products that demonstrate achievement in one or more of **five criteria areas**:

1. **Climate health**
2. **Human health**
3. **Ecosystem health**
4. **Social health and equity**
5. **Circular economy**

### Product Achievement Levels

There are **three achievement levels** for products:

| Level | Description | Multiplier |
|-------|-------------|------------|
| Level 1 | A first step toward sustainability for a criteria area. Widespread achievement drives market transformation. | 1x |
| Level 2 | Leadership position in the marketplace. Products are optimized and demonstrate a level of sustainability that peers aspire to achieve. | 2x |
| Level 3 | Elite products representing the forefront of sustainability. | 3x |

**Multi-attribute products:** Products that achieve **two or more criteria areas** are considered **multi-attribute**. Products that achieve higher levels of use and/or that are across additional criteria areas will be given a higher value in credit calculations.

### Eligible Product Categories

- Paints and coatings
- Adhesives and sealants
- Flooring
- Walls
- Ceilings
- Insulation
- Furniture
- Composite wood
- Plumbing fixtures

### Achievement Documentation

Achievement is demonstrated through **eligible compliant manufacturer product documentation**, which includes:
- Third-party product certifications
- Ecolabels
- Declarations
- Standards

A single product document can demonstrate multiple benefits and/or achievement levels, or the product can earn multi-attribute criteria through a combination of separate eligible product documents.

### Scoring Calculation

**Product Multi-Attribute Score (MAS):**

Eligible products meet the achievement levels and are scored as 1, 2, or 3. These scores are added across criteria areas to add up to a **maximum score of 5 per product**.

**Formula:**
```
Product value x MAS = Adjusted product value for LEED
```

**Equation 1: Calculate the multi-attribute adjusted value of a product category**

```
Product category adjusted value for LEED = 100 x [Sum of (Product MAS x Product value) for all products / (Total value of all products in the product category)]
```

Any product category adjusted value for LEED that **exceeds 100% earns 1 point**.

### Points Table

#### Table 1: Points for Multi-Attribute Achievement of Product Categories

| Number of Product Categories | Points |
|------------------------------|--------|
| 1 product category | 1 |
| 2 product categories | 2 |
| 3 product categories | 3 |
| 4 product categories | 4 |
| 5 product categories | 5 |

## Documentation Required

1. Product inventory for all eligible product categories
2. Manufacturer documentation demonstrating achievement in criteria areas (third-party certifications, ecolabels, declarations, standards)
3. Product value calculations (cost, area, volume, or unit)
4. MAS calculations for each product
5. Product category adjusted value calculations per Equation 1

## Data to Submit to USGBC

- Product category documentation showing achievement levels
- MAS scores for each product
- Calculated product category adjusted values
- Proof of third-party certifications/ecolabels

## Calculations Required

1. **MAS per product** = Sum of achievement level scores across all applicable criteria areas (max 5)
2. **Adjusted product value** = Product value x MAS
3. **Product category adjusted value** = 100 x Sum(MAS x Product value) / Total category value
4. **Points earned** = Number of product categories exceeding 100% adjusted value

## Referenced Standards

- Various third-party certifications and ecolabels (specific standards depend on selected products)
- ISO 14025 (for Type III EPDs)
- ISO 14024 (for Type I ecolabels)
- ISO 14021 (for Type II self-declared environmental claims)

# MRc5: CONSTRUCTION AND DEMOLITION WASTE DIVERSION

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Credit ID** | MRc5 |
| **Type** | Credit |
| **New Construction** | 1-2 points |
| **Core and Shell** | 1-2 points |
| **Impact Area** | Decarbonization, Quality of Life, Ecological Conservation and Restoration |

## Intent

To reduce construction and demolition waste disposed of in landfills and incineration facilities and pollution to the environment. To reduce the environmental impacts and embodied carbon of manufacturing new materials and products. To delay the need for new landfill facilities that are often located in frontline communities. To create green jobs and materials markets for building construction services.

## Requirements

### Achievement Pathways

**New Construction and Core and Shell:**

Must comply with BOTH of the following:

#### 1. Construction and Demolition Materials Management Plan

Develop and implement a **construction and demolition (C&D) materials management plan** and achieve points through diversion and recycling.

#### 2. Diversion (1-2 points)

Follow the materials management plan and provide a **final waste management report** detailing all waste generated, including disposal and diversion rates for the project. Calculations can be by **weight or volume but must be consistent throughout**.

**Diversion Strategies:**
- Off-site salvage
- Source-separation for single-material recycling
- Mixed C&D recycling
- Industry/manufacturer take-back programs

---

### Diversion Calculation Rules

#### Source-Separated Materials (100% Diverted)

The following are considered **100% diverted** for credit calculation purposes:

| Material Destination | Diversion Rate |
|---------------------|----------------|
| Recovered materials sent to a single-material recycler | 100% |
| Recovered materials sent for off-site salvage/reuse | 100% |
| Materials sent to a qualifying manufacturer or industry take-back program | 100% |
| Salvaged materials (recovered materials sent off-site for reuse) | **200%** (valued at twice the diversion rate) |

**Note:** Materials reused on-site contribute to **MRc1: Building and Materials Reuse**.

#### Mixed C&D Materials

- Mixed C&D materials sent to a processing facility for recovery must take the **facility average recycling rate**.
- Recycling rates **not verified by a third party** must assume a **maximum of 35% diversion rate**.

#### Excluded Materials (0% Diverted)

- Materials destined for **alternative daily cover** or **incineration/energy recovery** are considered **waste (0% diverted)**.
- **Hazardous waste** must be excluded from calculations.
- **On-site reuse** must be excluded from credit calculations (include in MRc1: Building and Materials Reuse).
- **Excavated soil and land-clearing debris** must be excluded from calculations.

---

### Points Table

#### Table 1: Points for C&D Diversion

| Thresholds | Points |
|------------|--------|
| Divert at least **50%** of the total construction and demolition material. At least **10%** of diverted materials must be salvaged or source-separated and sent to single-material recycler(s). | 1 |
| Divert at least **75%** of the total construction and demolition material. At least **25%** of the total diverted materials must be salvaged or source-separated and sent to single-material recycler(s). | 2 |

**Core and Shell only:** Include the building's approved construction and demolition waste management plan in the tenant guidelines.

## Documentation Required

1. Construction and demolition materials management plan
2. Final waste management report with:
   - Total waste generated (by weight or volume)
   - Waste diversion rates by material type
   - Waste disposal rates
3. Documentation of diversion strategies used
4. Receipts/records from recyclers, salvage facilities, and processing facilities
5. Facility average recycling rate documentation (for mixed C&D)
6. Third-party verification of recycling rates (if claiming above 35% for mixed C&D)
7. **Core and Shell:** Evidence that C&D waste management plan is included in tenant guidelines

## Data to Submit to USGBC

- C&D materials management plan
- Final waste management report
- Total waste generated and diverted (consistent units - weight or volume)
- Documentation supporting diversion claims:
  - Hauler tickets/receipts
  - Facility recycling rate certifications
  - Salvage documentation
- Calculation demonstrating overall diversion percentage
- Evidence that source-separated/salvaged thresholds are met (10% for 1 point, 25% for 2 points)

## Calculations Required

- **Total C&D Waste Generated** = Sum of all waste materials generated by the project
- **Total Diverted** = Sum of (material quantity x diversion rate for each material/destination)
- **Overall Diversion %** = Total Diverted / Total C&D Waste Generated x 100
- **Source-Separated/Salvaged %** = Quantity of source-separated + salvaged materials / Total Diverted x 100 (for 1 point) OR / Total C&D Waste x 100 (for 2 points threshold check)

**Note:** Salvaged materials count at 200% for diversion calculations.

## Referenced Standards

- None specifically cited (relies on project documentation and waste hauler/facility records)

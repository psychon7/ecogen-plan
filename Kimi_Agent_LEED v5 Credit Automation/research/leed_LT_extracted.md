# LTc1 - Sensitive Land Protection

## Basic Information

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Credit ID** | LTc1 |
| **Credit Name** | Sensitive Land Protection |
| **New Construction Points** | 1 point |
| **Core and Shell Points** | 1 point |
| **Impact Area Alignment** | Ecological Conservation and Restoration |
| **Applicability** | New Construction AND Core and Shell |

---

## Intent

To cultivate community resilience by avoiding the development of environmentally sensitive lands that provide critical ecosystem services and reduce the environmental impact from the location of a building on a site.

---

## Achievement Pathways

The credit offers two mutually exclusive options:

| Option | Title | Points (NC) | Points (CS) |
|--------|-------|-------------|-------------|
| Option 1 | Previously Developed Sites | 1 | 1 |
| Option 2 | Previously Undeveloped Sites | 1 | 1 |

---

## Option 1. Previously Developed Sites (1 point)

**Requirement:** Locate the development footprint on land that has been previously developed.

**Key Definition - Previously Developed:**
- Land that has been previously developed means land that has been altered by paving, construction, and/or land use that would typically have required regulatory permitting to have been initiated (alterations does not include tilling or maintenance activities related to landscaping or grounds maintenance).

---

## Option 2. Previously Undeveloped Sites (1 point)

**Requirement:** Locate the development footprint on land that does **NOT** meet the following criteria for sensitive land:

### Sensitive Land Criteria (ALL must be avoided):

1. **Prime Farmland**
   - Prime farmland, unique farmland, or farmland of statewide or local importance as defined by the U.S. Code of Federal Regulations, Title 7, Volume 6, Parts 400 to 699, Section 657.5 (or local equivalent for projects outside the U.S.)
   - Identified in a state Natural Resources Conservation Service (NRCS) soil survey (or local equivalent for projects outside the U.S.)

2. **Floodplains**
   - A flood hazard area shown on a legally adopted flood hazard map
   - OR otherwise legally designated by the local jurisdiction or the state
   - For projects without legally adopted flood hazard maps: the site must be entirely outside any floodplain subject to a 1% or greater chance of flooding in any given year (100-year floodplain)

3. **Notable Habitat** (land identified as habitat for one or more of the following):
   - Species listed as threatened or endangered under the U.S. Endangered Species Act or the state's endangered species act
   - Species or ecological communities classified by NatureServe as:
     - GH (possibly extinct)
     - G1 (critically imperiled)
     - G2 (imperiled)
   - Species listed as threatened or endangered under local equivalent standards (for projects outside the U.S.) that are not covered by NatureServe data

4. **Water Bodies**
   - Areas on or within 100 feet (30 meters) of a water body
   - Exception: minor improvements

5. **Wetlands**
   - Areas on or within 50 feet (15 meters) of a wetland
   - Exception: minor improvements

6. **Steep Slopes** (protect specified percentages from ALL development and construction activity):
   - For unstable, undeveloped slopes between 15% and 25%: protect **40%** of the steep slope area on the site
   - For unstable, undeveloped slopes steeper than 25%: protect **60%** of the steep slope area on the site

---

## Documentation Requirements for USGBC Submission

| Document | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| Site plan / survey | Showing development footprint and previously developed status (Option 1) |
| NRCS soil survey | Documentation identifying farmland status (Option 2) |
| Floodplain maps | Legally adopted flood hazard maps or equivalent documentation (Option 2) |
| NatureServe data / local equivalent | Evidence of species/habitat status (Option 2) |
| Wetland delineation | Professional wetland survey showing setbacks (Option 2) |
| Topographic survey | Slope analysis with development exclusion zones (Option 2) |
| Water body documentation | Distance measurements to nearest water bodies (Option 2) |

---

## Key Data Fields, Thresholds, and Metrics

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Water body setback | 100 feet (30 meters) |
| Wetland setback | 50 feet (15 meters) |
| Moderate slope (15-25%) protection | 40% of area |
| Steep slope (>25%) protection | 60% of area |
| Floodplain avoidance | Outside 100-year (1% annual chance) floodplain |
| NatureServe categories to avoid | GH, G1, G2 |

# LTc2 - Equitable Development

## Basic Information

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Credit ID** | LTc2 |
| **Credit Name** | Equitable Development |
| **New Construction Points** | 1-2 points |
| **Core and Shell Points** | 1-2 points |
| **Impact Area Alignment** | Quality of Life |
| **Applicability** | New Construction AND Core and Shell |
| **Project Type Variations** | Schools, Data Centers/Warehouses/Distribution Centers have specific pathways |

---

## Intent

To support the economic and social vitality of communities, provide opportunities for community members to live and work in close proximity, encourage project locations in areas with developmental challenges and promote the ecology, culture, and health of the surrounding area.

---

## Achievement Pathways Overview

| Option | Path | Title | Points |
|--------|------|-------|--------|
| Option 1 | Path 1 | Brownfield Remediation | 2 points |
| Option 1 | Path 2 | Historic Location | 1 point |
| Option 2 | Path 1 | Support Local Economy | 1 point |
| Option 2 | Path 2 | Location-Efficient Affordable Housing | 2 points |
| Option 3 | N/A | Equitable Construction | 2 points |
| Option 4 | Path 1 | Public Use Spaces | 1 point |
| Option 4 | Path 2 | Community Partnership | 1 point |
| Option 5 | N/A | Sensitive Project Location | Required for DC/WD/DC |

**Note:** Options may be combined. Maximum total: 2 points (NC and CS).

---

## Option 1. Priority Sites

### Path 1. Brownfield Remediation (2 points)

**Requirements:**
- Locate the project on a brownfield where soil or groundwater contamination has been identified
- The local, state, or national authority (whichever has jurisdiction) requires its remediation
- For voluntary remediation: provide confirmation by the local, state, or national authority verifying that the site is a brownfield
- Perform remediation to the satisfaction of the relevant authority

**Documentation:** Brownfield designation letter from jurisdictional authority; remediation completion documentation

### Path 2. Historic Location (1 point)

**Requirements:**
- Locate the project in a historic district, identified by the local government, based on a growth management plan or policy

**Documentation:** Local government designation of historic district; growth management plan or policy reference

---

## Option 2. Housing and Jobs Proximity

### Path 1. Support Local Economy (1 point)

**Requirements:**
- Employ individuals that live within the **administrative district of the project site** for **15%** of the construction jobs created by the LEED project

**Key Metric:** 15% local employment (by jobs, not hours)

**Documentation:** Employment records showing residential addresses of construction workers; administrative district boundary map

### Path 2. Location-Efficient Affordable Housing (2 points)

**Requirements:**
For residential or mixed-use projects, include a proportion of new affordable rental and/or for-sale dwelling units priced for households earning less than the area median income (AMI).

**Key Conditions:**
- Rental units must be maintained at affordable levels for a minimum of **15 years**
- Existing dwelling units are **exempt** from requirement calculations
- Projects must meet or exceed requirements mandated through inclusionary zoning by local jurisdictions

**Table 1. Minimum Affordable Units:**

| Unit Type | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| Rental dwelling units | At least 10% of project's total residential floor area, priced for up to 60% AMI |
| For-sale dwelling units | At least 10% of project's total residential floor area, priced for up to 80% AMI |

**AND** the project must achieve **one** of the following:
- Meet LTc3 Option 2 (Access to Transit) for 2 points
- Meet LTc3 Option 3 (Walkable Location) for 2 points
- Locate in a community where the jobs-to-housing ratio exceeds 1:2 within 0.5 miles (800 meters) walking distance

**Documentation:** AMI calculations from HUD or local equivalent; lease/sale agreements showing affordability restrictions; transit or walkability documentation

---

## Option 3. Equitable Construction (2 points)

**Requirements:** Provide access to workforce development training for construction workers through ONE of the following:

### Path A. Job-Related Skills Training
- On-the-job training in a Department of Labor registered apprenticeship program (or local equivalent outside U.S.)
- **15% or more** of total project construction hours performed by participants enrolled in registered apprenticeship programs

### Path B. Life-Skills Training
- Programs for construction workers conducted by an organization or government entity ON the construction site
- Topics: financial literacy, debt management, first-time home buying, entrepreneurship training
- **One course per month** for the duration of construction

**Documentation:** Apprenticeship registration records (Path A); training schedules and attendance records (Path B)

---

## Option 4. Equitable Access to Resources (SCHOOLS ONLY)

### Path 1. Public Use Spaces (1 point)

**Requirements:** In collaboration with school authorities, ensure at least **three** of the following types of spaces are accessible to and available for shared use by the public:
- Auditorium
- Gymnasium
- Cafeteria
- One or more classrooms
- Playing fields and stadiums
- Joint parking

**AND:** Provide access to toilets in joint-use areas after normal school hours.

### Path 2. Community Partnership (1 point)

**Requirements:** In collaboration with school authorities, contract with the community or other organizations to provide at least **two types** of dedicated-use spaces in the building, such as:
- Commercial office
- Health clinic
- Community service centers (provided by state or local offices)
- Library or media center
- Parking lot
- One or more commercial businesses

**AND:** Provide access to toilets in joint-use areas after normal school hours.

---

## Project Type Variations

### Schools (1-2 points)
- Meet Options 1, 2, and/or 3 (1-2 points)
- AND may also meet Option 4 (Equitable Access to Resources)

### Data Centers, Warehouses, and Distribution Centers (1-2 points)
- Meet Options 1, 2, and/or 3 (1-2 points)
- AND must meet **Option 5. Sensitive Project Location**:
  - Locate the project building a minimum of **300 feet (90 meters)** away from the property lines of the nearest sensitive receptors
  - Sensitive receptors include: residential areas, schools, day care centers, places of worship, hospitals, community centers, and public parks

---

## Key Data Fields, Thresholds, and Metrics

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Local employment threshold | 15% of construction jobs |
| Affordable rental units | 10% of residential floor area, up to 60% AMI |
| Affordable for-sale units | 10% of residential floor area, up to 80% AMI |
| Affordability period | 15 years minimum |
| Apprenticeship hours threshold | 15% of total construction hours |
| Life-skills training frequency | 1 course per month |
| Jobs-to-housing ratio threshold | > 1:2 within 0.5 miles |
| Sensitive receptor setback (DC/WD/DC) | 300 feet (90 meters) |
| Public use spaces required | 3 of 6 types |
| Community partnership spaces required | 2 types |

---

## Documentation Requirements for USGBC Submission

| Document | Applicable Options |
|----------|-------------------|
| Brownfield designation letter | Option 1, Path 1 |
| Remediation completion certificate | Option 1, Path 1 |
| Historic district designation document | Option 1, Path 2 |

# LTc3 - Compact and Connected Development

## Basic Information

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Credit ID** | LTc3 |
| **Credit Name** | Compact and Connected Development |
| **New Construction Points** | 1-6 points |
| **Core and Shell Points** | 1-6 points |
| **Impact Area Alignment** | Decarbonization, Quality of Life |
| **Applicability** | New Construction AND Core and Shell |
| **Project Type Variations** | Schools, Data Centers/Warehouses/Distribution Centers, Healthcare |

---

## Intent

To conserve land and ecosystem resources by encouraging development in areas with existing infrastructure. To promote livability, walkability, and transportation efficiency, including reduced vehicle distance traveled and associated emissions.

---

## Achievement Pathways Overview - New Construction / Core and Shell

| Option | Title | Points Range |
|--------|-------|-------------|
| Option 1 | Surrounding Density | 1-2 points |
| Option 2 | Access to Transit | 1-4 points |
| Option 3 | Walkable Location | 1-3 points |

Options may be combined. Maximum total: 6 points (NC and CS).

---

## Option 1. Surrounding Density (1-2 points)

**Requirement:** Locate on a site where the surrounding existing density within 0.25 miles (400 meters) offset of the project boundary meets the values in Table 1.

**Method:** Use either the separate residential and nonresidential densities OR the combined density values.

**Table 1. Points for Average Existing Density within 0.25 miles (400 Meters):**

| Points | Combined Density (SF/acre buildable land) | Combined Density (SM/hectare buildable land) | Residential Density (DU/acre) | Residential Density (DU/hectare) | Nonresidential Density (FAR) |
|--------|------------------------------------------|----------------------------------------------|-------------------------------|----------------------------------|------------------------------|
| 1 | 22,000 | 5,050 | 7 | 17.5 | 0.5 |
| 2 | 35,000 | 8,035 | 12 | 30 | 0.8 |

**Note:** DU = dwelling unit; FAR = floor area ratio. The counted density must be existing density, not zone density.

**Documentation:** Density calculations with source data; GIS analysis or survey showing development within 0.25-mile radius; buildable land area calculation

---

## Option 2. Access to Transit (1-4 points)

### Path 1. Public Transit Service (1-4 points)

**Proximity Requirements:** Locate any functional entry of the project within:
- **0.25 miles (400 meters)** walking distance of existing or planned bus, streetcar, or informal transit stops
- **OR 0.5 miles (800 meters)** walking distance of existing or planned bus rapid transit stops, passenger rail stations (light, heavy, or commuter rail), or commuter ferry terminals

**Trip Minimums (Table 2):**

| Points | Weekday Trips | Weekend Trips |
|--------|--------------|---------------|
| 1 | 72 | 30 |
| 2 | 132 | 78 |
| 3 | 160 | 120 |
| 4 | 360 | 216 |

**Counting Rules:**
- Both weekday and weekend trip minimums must be met
- For each qualifying transit route, only trips in ONE direction are counted
- If service varies by day:
  - Weekday trips: count the weekday with the LOWEST number of trips
  - Weekend trips: only count the weekend day with the HIGHEST number of trips
- If a qualifying transit route has multiple stops within the required walking distance, only trips from ONE stop are counted
- Planned stops and stations may count if: sited, funded, and under construction by the date of the certificate of occupancy; AND complete within 24 months of that date

### Path 2. Project-Sponsored Transit Service (1-2 points)

**Requirements:**
- Commit to providing year-round transit service (vans, shuttles, or buses) for regular occupants and visitors
- Service must provide transportation between the project site and external destinations (residential areas, public transportation stations)
- Must be guaranteed for at least **3 years** from the project's certificate of occupancy

**Table 3. Minimum Daily Project-Sponsored Transit Service:**

| Service Type | Total Daily Trips | Points |
|-------------|-------------------|--------|
| Providing shuttles | 30 | 1 |
| (implied higher threshold) | Higher threshold | 2 |

**Additional Requirements:**
- Provide at least one accessible transit stop shelter within 0.25 miles (400 meters) walking distance from a functional entry
- For each qualifying transit route, total trips (inbound AND outbound) are counted
- If a qualifying transit route has multiple stops within the required walking distance, only trips from ONE stop are counted

**Documentation:** Transit schedule documentation; walking distance calculations; service guarantee agreement; shelter location documentation

---

## Option 3. Walkable Location (1-3 points)

**Requirement:** Locate on a site that meets the location efficiency requirements via Walk Score OR has proximity to existing and publicly available uses within 0.5 miles (800 meters) walking distance from any functional entry.

**Table 4. Points for Location Efficiency:**

| Walk Score | Proximity to Uses (number of types) | Points |
|-----------|-------------------------------------|--------|
| 60-69 | 4-7 | 1 |
| 70-79 | 8-10 | 2 |
| 80 or more | >= 11 | 3 |

**Counting Restrictions for Proximity to Uses:**
- A use may be counted as only ONE use type (e.g., a retail store may be counted only once even if it sells products in several categories)
- No more than TWO uses in each type of use may be counted (e.g., if five restaurants are within walking distance, only two may be counted)
- The counted uses must represent at least **three of the five categories**

**Documentation:** Walk Score report OR inventory of uses within 0.5-mile walking distance with use type categorization

---

## Project Type Variations

### Schools (1-6 points)
- Meet Options 1, 2, and/or 3 (1-6 points)

**AND/OR:**

### Option 4. Surrounding Density and Development (1-2 points) - SCHOOLS

**Path 1. Surrounding Density (1-2 points):**
Meet Option 1 Surrounding Density requirements in New Construction.

**Path 2. Connected Site (1-2 points):**
Locate the project on a previously developed site that also meets one of the connected site conditions:

| Type of Site | Description | Points |
|-------------|-------------|--------|
| Adjacent site | At least a contiguous 25% of the project boundary borders parcels that are previously developed sites | 1 |
| Infill site | At least 75% of the project boundary borders parcels that are previously developed sites | 2 |

**Note:** Bordering rights-of-way do NOT constitute previously developed land; it is the status of the property on the other side of the right-of-way that contributes to the calculation. Any part of the boundary that borders a water body is excluded from the calculation.

### Option 5. Access to Transit or Pedestrian Access (1-4 points) - SCHOOLS

**Path 1. Access to Transit (1-4 points):**
Meet Option 2, Access to Transit, listed above.

**Path 2. Pedestrian Access (1-2 points):**
Locate the project with an attendance boundary where dwelling units are:
- Within **0.75 miles (1,200 meters)** walking distance of a functional entry for students in eighth grade or below (ages 14 and below)
- Within **1.5 miles (2,400 meters)** walking distance of a functional entry for students in ninth grade or above (ages 15 and above)
- Provide pedestrian access to the site from ALL residential areas in the attendance boundary

**Table 6. Points for Dwelling Units Within Walking Distance:**

| Percentage of Dwelling Units in Attendance Boundary | Points |
|-----------------------------------------------------|--------|
| 50% | 1 |
| 60% | 2 |

---

### Data Centers, Warehouses, and Distribution Centers (1-6 points)
- Meet Options 1, 2, and/or 3 above (1-6 points)

**AND/OR:**

### Option 6. Surrounding Development and Resources (1-2 points) - DC/WD/DC

**Path 1. Development and Adjacency (1-2 points):**
Locate the project on a site that meets one of the site conditions in Table 7.

**Table 7. Points for Development and Adjacency:**

| Type of Site | Points |
|-------------|--------|
| Previously developed site that was used for industrial or commercial purposes | 1 |
| Previously developed and adjacent site with bordering parcels currently used for industrial or commercial purposes | 2 |

**Qualification Notes:**
- To qualify as an adjacent site: at least a contiguous 25% of the project boundary must border parcels that are previously developed sites
- Bordering rights-of-way do not constitute previously developed land
- Any part of the boundary that borders a water body is excluded from the calculation

**Path 2. Transportation Resources (1-2 points):**
Locate the project on a site that has:
- **2 of the following** for 1 point
- **All 4 of the following** for 2 points

1. The site is within a **10-mile (16-kilometer)** driving distance of a main logistics hub
2. The site is within a **one-mile (1,600 meter)** driving distance of an on-off ramp to a highway
3. The site is within a **one-mile (1,600 meter)** driving distance of an access point to an active freight rail line
4. The site is served by an active freight rail spur

**Note:** A planned transportation resource must be sited, funded, and under construction by the date of the certificate of occupancy and complete within 24 months of that date.

---

### Healthcare (1-6 points)
- Meet Options 1, 2, and/or 3 above

**AND/OR:**

### Option 7. Surrounding Density (1 point) - HEALTHCARE

Locate on a site where the surrounding existing density within 0.25 miles (400 meters) offset of the project boundary meets one of the following:
- At least **7 dwelling units per acre** (17.5 DU per hectare) with a **0.5 floor area ratio**
- At least **22,000 square feet per acre** (5,050 square meters per hectare) of buildable land

**Special Provision for Previously Developed Rural Healthcare Campus Sites:**
Achieve a minimum development density of **30,000 square feet per acre** (6,890 square meters per hectare).

# LTc4 - Transportation Demand Management

## Basic Information

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Credit ID** | LTc4 |
| **Credit Name** | Transportation Demand Management |
| **New Construction Points** | 1-4 points |
| **Core and Shell Points** | 1-4 points |
| **Impact Area Alignment** | Decarbonization, Quality of Life |
| **Applicability** | New Construction AND Core and Shell |

---

## Intent

To reduce pollution and land development effects from automobile use through encouraging alternative transportation networks. To promote more livable and healthy communities through reduced vehicle distance traveled and associated emissions.

---

## Achievement Pathways Overview

**REQUIRED FIRST:** Transportation Demand Assessment (TDA) -- must be completed before earning points from options

| Option | Path | Title | Points |
|--------|------|-------|--------|
| Option 1 | Path 1 | Reduce Parking | 1-3 points |
| Option 1 | Path 2 | Parking Fee | 2 points |
| Option 2 | Path 1 | Bicycle Network and Storage | 1 point |
| Option 2 | Path 2 | Shower and Changing Facilities | 1 point |
| Option 2 | Path 3 | Bicycle Maintenance | 1 point |

Maximum total: 4 points (NC and CS).

---

## Transportation Demand Assessment (REQUIRED)

**Purpose:** Must be completed AND must implement one or more options for up to 4 points.

**Assessment Requirements:**
Assess the number of VMT (Vehicle Miles Traveled) and carbon emissions associated with regular building occupants' travel to and from the project building. Must include:

1. Estimate the **annual VMT**
2. Estimate **annual baseline case** for carbon emissions
3. Assess **low-carbon transportation options**
4. Estimate **annual proposed case** for carbon emissions
5. Estimate the **total reduction of carbon emissions** between annual baseline case and annual proposed case

**Exemptions from TDA:**
The following projects are exempt from the TDA requirements:
- Projects participating in a local or regional government-mandated TDM program
- Residential affordable housing projects in an infill location
- Office, mixed-use, residential, or retail projects located within a transit priority area
- Projects within a walking distance of 0.5 miles (800 meters) to an existing or planned major transit stop

**Documentation:** VMT analysis report; carbon emissions baseline and proposed case calculations; TDM program enrollment (if exempt)

---

## Option 1. Parking (1-3 points)

### Path 1. Reduce Parking (1-3 points)

**Requirement:** Provide a reduction in parking capacity using base ratios from:
- Institute of Transportation Engineers (ITE) Parking Generation Manual, **sixth edition**, OR
- A comparable resource applied by a qualified transportation engineer or planner, OR
- Supplementary LEED guidance

**Table 1. Points for Percentage of Reduced Parking Capacity:**

| Reduced Parking Percentage | Points |
|---------------------------|--------|
| 30% reduction from base ratios | 1 |
| 60% reduction from base ratios | 2 |
| 100% reduction from base ratios (no parking) | 3 |

**Documentation:** Parking study by qualified transportation professional; ITE Parking Generation Manual reference with base ratios; existing parking capacity calculation

### Path 2. Parking Fee (2 points)

**Requirement:** Implement a daily, monthly, or annual parking fee at a cost equal to or greater than the **local market rate** for public or private parking.

**Documentation:** Parking fee schedule; market rate analysis from comparable parking facilities

---

## Option 2. Active Travel Facilities (1-3 points)

### Path 1. Bicycle Network and Storage (1 point)

**BICYCLE NETWORK REQUIREMENT:**
Design or locate the project such that a functional entry and/or bicycle storage is within a **600-foot (180-meter)** walking distance or bicycling distance of a bicycle network meeting:
- Provides a contiguous network that spans at least **3 miles (4,800 meters)**
- Consists of bicycle paths, lanes, multiuse trails, or streets with maximum speed limit of **25 mph (40 kph)**
- Sidewalks where local code permits bicycles are acceptable
- Planned bicycle trails or lanes may count if fully funded by certificate of occupancy date and scheduled for completion within **3 years**

**SCHOOLS ADDENDUM:** Provide dedicated bicycle lanes extending from the student bike-parking location to at least the end of the school property without any barriers (e.g., fences on school property).

**BICYCLE STORAGE REQUIREMENT:**

Provide short-term AND long-term bicycle storage:

**Short-term storage:** Within 600 feet (180 meters) walking distance to any main entrance, but no fewer than **4 spaces per building**

**Long-term storage:** Within 300 feet (90 meters) walking distance from any functional entry, but no fewer than **4 spaces per building**, in addition to short-term storage

**Table 2. Number of Spaces Required for Short- and Long-Term Bicycle Storage:**

| Project Type | Short-Term Storage | Long-Term Storage |
|-------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Commercial, Institutional, Schools, Healthcare | At least 2.5% of all peak visitors but no fewer than 4 spaces per building | At least 5% of all regular building occupants but no fewer than 4 storage spaces per building, in addition to short-term storage |
| Residential | (see mixed-use) | At least 15% of all regular building occupants but no fewer than 1 storage space per 3 dwelling units, in addition to short-term storage spaces |
| Mixed Use | Meet storage requirements for nonresidential and residential portions separately | Meet storage requirements for nonresidential and residential portions separately |
| Retail | At least 2 short-term bicycle storage spaces for every 5,000 SF (465 SM) but no fewer than 2 storage spaces per building | At least 5% of regular building occupants but no fewer than 2 storage spaces per building in addition to short-term bicycle storage spaces |

**Shared Micromobility Provision:**
Shared micromobility storage, bicycle-sharing stations, and/or publicly available bicycle parking may be counted for up to **50%** of the required short-term and long-term storage space if:
- It meets the maximum allowable walking distance
- It is not double counted (short-term and long-term counted separately)
- The storage location is communicated to building occupants and visitors

**Special Exclusions:**
- **School projects:** Can exclude students in third grade and younger from the regular building occupant count for long-term storage
- **Healthcare projects:** Can exclude patients from the regular building occupant count for long-term storage

### Path 2. Shower and Changing Facilities (1 point)

**Standard Requirement:**
Provide access to on-site showers with changing facilities for **1% of all regular building occupants**.

Off-site showers acceptable if they meet the needs of all occupants and are within **0.25 miles (400 meters)** walking distance.

**Large Occupancy Projects (scaling formula):**
- Provide at least one on-site shower for the first **100** regular building occupants
- One additional shower for every **150** regular building occupants thereafter, up to **999** regular building occupants
- After 999:
  - One additional shower for every **500** regular building occupants, for additional 1,000-4,999
  - One additional shower for every **1,000** regular building occupants, for additional 5,000+

### Path 3. Bicycle Maintenance (1 point)

**Requirement:** Provide a permanent and secure bicycle repair station that includes:
- A complete set of tools
- An air pump securely fastened to the repair stand
- Located in the area dedicated to long-term bicycle storage

# LTc5 - Electric Vehicles

## Basic Information

| Field | Detail |

| **Core and Shell Points** | 1-2 points |

---

## Intent

To encourage the use of EVs and infrastructure. To help reduce the negative health effects on communities by lowering greenhouse gas emissions and other pollutants emitted from conventionally fueled cars and trucks.

---

## Achievement Pathways Overview

| Option | Title | Points Range |
|--------|-------|-------------|
| Option 1 | Electric Vehicle Supply Equipment (EVSE) | 1-2 points |
| Option 2 | Electric Vehicle Readiness | 1 point |

Options may be combined. Maximum total: 2 points (NC and CS).

---

## Option 1. Electric Vehicle Supply Equipment (EVSE) (1-2 points)

**EVSE Technical Requirements:**
All EVSE must meet the following criteria:

1. Provide **Level 2 or Level 3** charging capacity per manufacturer's requirements and National Electrical Code (NFPA 70)
2. Provide **208-240 volts or greater** for each required space
3. Meet the connected functionality criteria for ENERGY STAR-certified EVSE and be capable of responding to time-of-use market signals (e.g., price)
4. At least one EV charging station has an **accessible parking space**:
   - At least **9 feet (2.5 meters)** wide
   - With a **5-foot (1.5-meter)** access aisle
   - Accessibility features for use by people with mobility, ambulatory, and visual limitations

**Table 1. Points for Installed EVSE (Percentage of Total Parking Spaces):**

### Commercial Projects

| EVSE Parking Threshold | Points |
|------------------------|--------|
| 5% or at least 2 spaces, whichever is greater | 1 |
| 10% or at least 4 spaces, whichever is greater | 2 |

### Residential Projects

| EVSE Parking Threshold | Points |
|------------------------|--------|
| 10% or at least 5 spaces, whichever is greater | 1 |
| 15% or at least 10 spaces, whichever is greater | 2 |

---

## Option 2. Electric Vehicle Readiness (1 point)

**Requirement:** Make the minimum number of total parking spaces EV-ready as specified in Table 2.

**EV-Ready Parking Space Technical Requirements:**
Must provide a **full-circuit installation** including:
- **208-240 volts**
- **40-amp** panel capacity
- A **conduit (raceway)** with wiring that terminates in a junction box or charging outlet

**Important Restriction:**
Any space with an installed EVSE counted for credit under Option 1 may **NOT** be counted for credit as an EV-ready space under Option 2.

**Table 2. Points for EV-Ready Parking (Percentage of Total Parking Spaces):**

| Project Type | Threshold | Points |
|-------------|-----------|--------|
| Commercial | At least 10% or at least 10 spaces, whichever is greater | 1 |
| Residential | At least 20% or at least 20 spaces, whichever is greater | 1 |

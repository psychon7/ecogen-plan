## WEp1: Water Metering and Reporting

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Credit ID** | WEp1 |
| **Name** | Water Metering and Reporting |
| **Type** | Prerequisite (Required) |
| **Applicability** | New Construction, Core and Shell |
| **Points** | N/A (Required) |
| **Impact Area** | Ecological Conservation and Restoration |

### Intent
To conserve potable water resources, support water management, and identify opportunities for additional water savings by tracking water consumption.

### Requirements

#### Achievement Pathway: Water Metering
**Applicable to:** New Construction and Core and Shell

Install or use existing permanent water meters to monitor, record, and report the total water consumption for potable and alternative water sources for the building and associated grounds. Report whole-project use for each type of water source supplied to the building and associated grounds, with the following additional provisions:

1. **Access:** The facility manager and/or tenant(s) must be able to access the meter data.
2. **Alternative Water Metering:** Meter alternative water sources separately from municipally supplied potable water.
3. **USGBC Reporting Commitment:** Commit to sharing with USGBC the resulting whole-project water usage data at least annually. This commitment must carry forward for five years or until the building changes ownership or lessee.

**Scope Flexibility:** The requirements may be applied to the project scope of work and exclude future tenant utility services and submeters that will be installed in the tenant scope of work.

### Documentation Required for USGBC Submission
- Proof of installation (or existing) permanent water meter(s)
- Documentation showing meter can monitor, record, and report total water consumption
- Documentation of separate metering for potable vs. alternative water sources
- Signed commitment letter for annual water usage data sharing with USGBC (5-year commitment)
- Confirmation that facility manager/tenant can access meter data

### Key Data Points
- Total water consumption (potable + alternative)
- Separate readings for potable and alternative water sources
- Annual reporting to USGBC for 5 years minimum

### Referenced Standards
- None specifically referenced (beyond USGBC reporting requirements)

## WEp2: Minimum Water Efficiency

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Credit ID** | WEp2 |
| **Name** | Minimum Water Efficiency |
| **Type** | Prerequisite (Required) |
| **Applicability** | New Construction, Core and Shell |
| **Points** | N/A (Required) |
| **Impact Area** | Decarbonization, Ecological Conservation and Restoration |

### Intent
To reduce potable water consumption and the associated energy consumption and carbon emissions required to treat and distribute water, and to preserve potable water resources through an efficiency-first approach.

### Requirements Summary

Projects must meet ALL of the following three requirement areas:

1. **Minimum Fixture and Fittings Efficiency** (Option 1 OR Option 2)
2. **Minimum Equipment Water Efficiency** (Required)
3. **Minimum Outdoor Water Use Efficiency** (Option 1 OR Option 2)

### Core and Shell Special Note
These requirements must be met for the base building fixtures and fittings, appliances, equipment, process water, and outdoor water use. For tenant spaces, include manufacturer documentation for the base building's fixtures and fittings, appliances, and equipment in IPp4: Tenant Guidelines.

**Pressure Adjustment:** Projects located where standard supply pressure is different than the LEED baseline supply pressure may calculate the water consumption of flow fixtures and fittings at the local standard supply pressure.

---

### Requirement 1: Minimum Fixture and Fittings Efficiency

#### OPTION 1: PRESCRIPTIVE PATH — MAXIMUM FLUSH AND FLOW RATES

For all new and existing fixtures and fittings within the project boundary, do not exceed the maximum flush and flow rates listed in **Table 1**.

**Table 1. Maximum Installed Flush or Flow Rates for Prescriptive Path**

| Fixture or Fitting | Maximum Installed Flush or Flow Rate (IP) | Maximum Installed Flush or Flow Rate (SI) |
|---|---|---|
| Toilet (water closet) | 1.28 gpf | 4.8 lpf |
| Urinal | 0.50 gpf | 1.9 lpf |
| Public lavatory (restroom) faucet | 0.50 gpm | 1.9 lpm |
| Private lavatory faucets | 1.50 gpm | 5.7 lpm |
| Kitchen faucet | 1.8 gpm | 6.8 lpm |
| Showerhead | 2.00 gpm | 7.6 lpm |

**Notes:**
- gpf = gallons per flush; gpm = gallons per minute; lpf = liters per flush; lpm = liters per minute
- WaterSense label is available for toilet, urinal, private lavatory faucets, and showerhead. WaterSense-labeled fixtures are **recommended** for projects located in the U.S. and Canada.
- For dual-flush toilets, the full-flush volume shall be equal to or fewer than 1.28 gpf/4.8 lpf; A **weighted average cannot be used**.

#### OPTION 2: PERFORMANCE PATH — CALCULATED REDUCTION

For all new and existing fixtures and fittings within the project boundary, **reduce aggregate water consumption by 20%** from the baseline listed in **Table 2**.

**Table 2. Baseline Water Consumption of Fixtures and Fittings**

| Fixture or Fitting | Baseline Installed Flush or Flow Rate (IP) | Baseline Installed Flush or Flow Rate (SI) |
|---|---|---|
| Toilet (water closet) | 1.6 gpf | 6.0 lpf |
| Urinal | 1.0 gpf | 3.8 lpf |
| Public lavatory (restroom) faucet | 0.50 gpm at 60 psi | 1.9 lpm at 415 kPa |
| Private lavatory faucets | 2.2 gpm at 60 psi | 8.3 lpm at 415 kPa |
| Kitchen faucet | 2.2 gpm at 60 psi | 8.3 lpm at 415 kPa |
| Showerhead | 2.5 gpm at 80 psi per shower stall | 9.5 lpm at 550 kPa per shower stall |

**Notes:**
- psi = pounds per square inch; kPa = kilopascals
- WaterSense label available for same fixtures as Option 1
- For dual-flush toilets, same rule applies: full-flush volume <= 1.28 gpf/4.8 lpf; weighted average cannot be used

**Calculation Method:**
- Calculate aggregate water consumption based on occupant usage patterns
- Compare proposed fixture performance against baseline in Table 2
- Must demonstrate minimum 20% reduction

### Requirement 3: Minimum Outdoor Water Use Efficiency

#### OPTION 1: NO IRRIGATION
Show that the landscape does not require a permanent irrigation system beyond a maximum two-year establishment period.

#### OPTION 2: EFFICIENT IRRIGATION
Reduce the project's irrigation water requirement by **at least 30%** from the calculated baseline for the site's annual **theoretical irrigation requirement (TIR)**. Reductions must be achieved through plant species selection and irrigation system efficiency, as calculated by the **TIR methodology outlined by the U.S. Environmental Protection Agency (EPA)**.

---

### Calculations Required

1. **Option 2 (Performance Path) - Fixture Water Reduction:**
   - Calculate baseline water consumption using Table 2 fixture flow/flush rates
   - Calculate proposed water consumption using installed fixture rates
   - Formula: % Reduction = (Baseline - Proposed) / Baseline x 100%
   - Must achieve >= 20% reduction

2. **Efficient Irrigation Calculation:**
   - Calculate site's annual Theoretical Irrigation Requirement (TIR) using EPA methodology
   - Calculate proposed irrigation requirement with plant selection and system efficiency
   - Must achieve >= 30% reduction from TIR baseline

### Documentation Required for USGBC Submission
- **Option 1 (Prescriptive):** Fixture cut sheets showing flush/flow rates meet Table 1 maximums
- **Option 2 (Performance):** Water use calculation showing 20% reduction from Table 2 baseline
- Appliance/equipment cut sheets showing ENERGY STAR or equivalent compliance
- Cooling tower specification sheets (if applicable) showing makeup water meters, conductivity controllers, overflow alarms, drift eliminator efficiency
- Process water documentation (no once-through cooling, tempering devices, etc.)
- **Option 1 (No Irrigation):** Landscape plan or documentation showing no permanent irrigation needed beyond 2-year establishment
- **Option 2 (Efficient Irrigation):** TIR calculation showing >= 30% reduction
- IPp4 Tenant Guidelines (C+S only) - manufacturer documentation for base building fixtures/fittings

### Referenced Standards
- **EPA WaterSense** (recommended for U.S. and Canada projects for labeled fixtures)
- **ENERGY STAR** (for appliances and commercial kitchen equipment)
- **EPA TIR Methodology** (for irrigation calculations)
- ASHRAE 90.1-2022 (referenced for point-of-use water heater criteria in C+S guidance)

## WEc1: Water Metering and Leak Detection

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Credit ID** | WEc1 |
| **Name** | Water Metering and Leak Detection |
| **Type** | Credit |
| **Applicability** | New Construction (1 point), Core and Shell (1 point) |
| **Points** | 1 point |
| **Impact Area** | Decarbonization, Quality of Life, Ecological Conservation and Restoration |

### Intent
To conserve potable water resources, support water management, limit potential material waste due to water leak damages, and identify opportunities for additional water savings by tracking water consumption.

### Requirements

#### Achievement Pathway: Option 1. Submeters (1 point)

Install permanent water meters for each applicable subsystem defined below:

**Required Subsystems (all projects):**
1. **Indoor plumbing fixtures and fittings** - Meter systems serving at least 80% of indoor fixtures and fittings as described in WEp2. Fixtures and fittings not addressed in the prerequisite (janitor sinks, water coolers, bottle fillers) may be included or excluded at the project team's discretion.
2. **Irrigation system** (if included in project scope)
3. **Each makeup water system** (e.g., cold water inlet for domestic hot water, swimming pools, chilled water systems, process water systems)
4. **Commercial kitchen** (if the kitchen serves at least 100 meals per day of operation)
5. **Laundry** (if the project includes commercial laundry equipment that processes at least 120,000 lb [57,606 kg] of laundry per year OR if the project includes a public laundry room)

**Data Access Requirements:**
- The facility manager and/or tenant(s) must be able to access the submeter data in real time via local network, building management system, cloud service, app, or online database
- All submeters must be capable of recording data at least hourly

**Core and Shell Additional Requirement:**
- In addition to the preceding requirements, meters must be installed for future tenant spaces so that tenants will be capable of independently metering water consumption in their spaces
- Provide enough meters to capture total potable water use with a minimum of one per floor

**Healthcare Additional Requirement:**
- In addition to the requirements above, install water meters in **any five** of the following:
  - Purified water systems (reverse-osmosis, deionized)
  - Filter backwash water
  - Water use in the dietary department
  - Water use in laundry
  - Water use in laboratory
  - Water use in central sterile and processing department
  - Water use in physiotherapy and hydrotherapy and treatment areas
  - Water use in surgical suite
  - Closed-loop hydronic system makeup water
  - Cold water makeup for domestic hot water systems
- If a healthcare project does not include five of the additional subsystems listed above within the project scope, the project may alternatively submeter all water subsystems that are applicable to the project scope.

**Residential Additional Requirement:**
- Install a permanent water meter for each residential dwelling unit that measures the total potable water use for the unit
- These meters need not be utility owned or utility grade

#### Achievement Pathway: Option 2. Leak Detection Sensors (1 point)

Install permanent water flow meters or sensors for each applicable subsystem defined below:

**Required Subsystems:**
1. **Project irrigation system** at the point of entry (if irrigation is included in the project scope)
2. **At least 50% of the project flush fixtures.** Water sensors can be installed on each flush fixture or for a group of flush fixtures (e.g., one per restroom facility). For Core and Shell projects, this only applies to flush fixtures within the project's scope of work.
3. **Each makeup water system** (e.g., cold water inlet for domestic hot water, swimming pools, chilled water systems, and process water systems)

**Leak Detection System Requirements:**
- The system must be able to identify a leak triggered by abnormal flow rate above normal range, OR physically detect a water leak
- Must initiate an alarm upon leak detection

**Data Access Requirements:**
- The facility manager and/or tenant(s) must be able to access the sensor data in real time via local network, BMS, cloud service, app, or online database

**Action Plan Requirement:**
- Develop an action plan that addresses:
  - How the building manager or tenant will have access to data in real time
  - How the building manager and/or tenant(s) will address and remedy any detected leak

### Calculations Required
- None specifically (must verify 80% coverage of fixtures for Option 1, 50% of flush fixtures for Option 2)

### Documentation Required for USGBC Submission
- **Option 1 (Submeters):**
  - Plumbing drawings showing submeter locations for each required subsystem
  - Specification sheets for submeters demonstrating hourly data recording capability
  - Documentation of real-time data access method (network, BMS, cloud, app, etc.)
  - C+S: Documentation of tenant meter infrastructure (min. one per floor)
  - Healthcare: Documentation of 5 (or all applicable) additional submetered systems
  - Residential: Documentation of individual dwelling unit meters

- **Option 2 (Leak Detection Sensors):**
  - Sensor layout drawings showing coverage of at least 50% of flush fixtures and all makeup water systems
  - Sensor specification sheets
  - Documentation of real-time data access method
  - Copy of leak detection action plan
  - Documentation showing alarm initiation capability

### Referenced Standards
- None specifically referenced

### OPTION 5: Optimize Process Water Use (1-2 points)

#### PATH 1: LIMIT COOLING TOWER CYCLES (1-2 points)

For cooling towers and evaporative condensers, conduct a **one-time potable water analysis**, measuring at least the five control parameters listed in **Table 8**.

**Table 8. Maximum Concentrations for Parameters in Condenser Water**

| Parameter | Maximum Level |
|---|---|
| Ca (as CaCO3) | 600 ppm |
| Total alkalinity | 500 ppm |
| SiO2 | 150 ppm |
| Cl- | 300 ppm |
| Conductivity | 3,300 uS/cm |

**Calculation Method:**
Calculate the maximum number of cooling tower cycles by dividing the maximum allowed concentration level of each parameter by the actual concentration level of each parameter found in the potable makeup water analysis. Limit cooling tower cycles to avoid exceeding maximum values for any of these parameters.

The materials of construction for the water system that contact the cooling tower water must be of the type that can operate and be maintained within the cycles established in **Table 9**.

**Table 9. Points for Cooling Tower Cycles (New Construction)**

| Cooling Tower Cycles | Points |
|---|---|
| Maximum number of cycles achieved without exceeding any maximum concentration levels or affecting operation of condenser water system | 1 |
| Meet the maximum calculated number of cycles to earn 1 point AND increase the number of cycles by a minimum of 25% by increasing the level of treatment and/or maintenance in condenser or makeup water systems | 2 |
| **OR** Meet the maximum calculated number of cycles to earn 1 point AND use a minimum 20% alternative water | 2 |

**District Cooling Note:** Projects whose cooling is provided by district cooling systems are eligible to achieve Path 1 if the district cooling system complies with the preceding requirements.

#### PATH 2: OPTIMIZE WATER USE FOR COOLING (1-2 points)

**Eligibility:** The baseline system designated for the building using ASHRAE 90.1-2019 or 90.1-2022, Appendix G, Table G3.1.1-3, must include a cooling tower (systems 7, 8, 11, 12, and 13).

Achieve increasing levels of cooling tower water efficiency beyond a water-cooled chiller system with axial variable-speed fan cooling towers having a maximum drift of 0.002% of recirculated water volume and three cooling tower cycles.

**Table 10. Points for Reducing Annual Water Use Compared to Water-Cooled Chiller System (New Construction)**

| Percentage Reduction | Points |
|---|---|
| 25% | 1 |
| 50% | 2 |

**District Cooling Note:** Projects whose cooling is provided by district cooling systems are eligible to achieve Path 2 if the district cooling system complies with the preceding requirements.

#### PATH 3: PROCESS WATER USE (1-2 points)

Demonstrate that the project is using:
- A minimum of **20% alternative water** to meet the process water demand = **1 point**
- A minimum of **30% alternative water** to meet the process water demand = **2 points**

Ensure that alternative water is of sufficient quality for its intended end use.

**Eligibility:** Process water uses eligible for achievement of Path 3 must represent at least 10% of total building regulated water use and may **not** include water used for cooling.

## Summary of Key Calculations Required for WEc2

### 1. Option 1: Whole-Project Water Use
- **Baseline Model:** Sum of all water uses (fixtures, fittings, appliances, process, irrigation) using baseline rates
- **Proposed Model:** Sum of all water uses with proposed efficiency measures and alternative water sources
- **Formula:** % Reduction = (Baseline - Proposed) / Baseline x 100%
- Must include all end uses for comprehensive accounting

### 2. Option 2: Fixture and Fittings Calculated Reduction
- Start from WEp2 baseline (Table 2)
- Calculate additional savings from more efficient fixtures and/or alternative water sources
- **Formula:** Same as WEp2 but targeting higher reduction thresholds (30%, 35%, 40%)

### 3. Option 4: Outdoor Water Use (TIR Calculation)
- Calculate Theoretical Irrigation Requirement (TIR) using EPA methodology
- Calculate proposed irrigation requirement with efficient plant selection and irrigation systems
- **Formula:** % Reduction = (TIR_baseline - TIR_proposed) / TIR_baseline x 100%

### 4. Option 5 Path 1: Cooling Tower Cycles
- Conduct one-time potable water analysis for 5 parameters (Ca, alkalinity, SiO2, Cl-, conductivity)
- Calculate maximum cycles for each parameter: Cycles_max_i = Limit_i / Actual_i
- System maximum cycles = MIN(all Cycles_max_i)
- Points based on achieving max cycles, increasing cycles 25% or 30%, and/or using alternative water

### 5. Option 5 Path 2: Optimize Water Use for Cooling
- Establish baseline as water-cooled chiller with axial variable-speed fan cooling towers (0.002% drift, 3 cycles)
- Calculate annual water use of proposed system vs. baseline
- **Formula:** % Reduction = (Baseline_annual - Proposed_annual) / Baseline_annual x 100%

### 6. Option 5 Path 3: Process Water Alternative Water
- Calculate total process water demand (excluding cooling)
- Calculate alternative water contribution
- **Formula:** % Alternative = Alternative_water / Total_process_water x 100%
- Must be >= 10% of total building regulated water use to be eligible

### IPp1: Climate Resilience Assessment

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Credit ID** | IPp1 |
| **Full Name** | Climate Resilience Assessment |
| **Type** | Prerequisite (Required) |
| **Points** | N/A |
| **New Construction** | Required |
| **Core and Shell** | Required |
| **Impact Area Alignment** | Quality of Life |

#### INTENT
To promote the comprehensive assessment of observed, projected, and future natural hazards for climate resilience with an aim to enhance awareness of hazards, increase transparency of risks, reduce vulnerabilities, and ensure long-term safety and sustainability.

#### REQUIREMENTS

**Achievement Pathways: New Construction and Core and Shell**

**Climate and Natural Hazard Assessment**

Complete a climate and natural hazard assessment. As part of the assessment, identify observed, projected, and future natural hazards that could potentially affect the project site and building function.

**Required Hazards to Address (Site-Specific Natural Hazards):**
The assessment must address the following hazards, including but not limited to:
- Drought
- Extreme heat
- Extreme cold
- Flooding
- Hurricanes and high winds
- Hail
- Landslides
- Sea level rise and storm surge
- Tornadoes
- Tsunamis
- Wildfires and smoke
- Winter storms
- Other relevant hazards (specify)

**Priority Hazards:**
Identify **two priority hazards at minimum** to address through proposed design strategies.

**For Each Priority Hazard, the project team must assess and specify the following:**

| Required Data Field | Description |
|---|---|
| IPCC Emissions Scenario | The Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change (IPCC) emissions scenario used, specifying the shared socioeconomic pathways |
| Projected Service Life | Projected service life of the LEED project (e.g., fiscal year 2050 or 100 years) |
| Hazard Level | Hazard level |
| Hazard Risk Rating | Hazard risk rating |
| Exposure | Exposure level |
| Sensitivity | Sensitivity level |
| Adaptive Capacity | Adaptive capacity level |
| Vulnerability | Vulnerability level |
| Overall Risk Level | Overall risk level |
| Potential Impact on Site and Building | Potential impact on the project site and building function |
| Potential Impact During Construction | Potential impact on the project site during construction |

**Follow-Up Requirement:**
Where possible, use the information from the assessment to inform the planning, design, and operations and maintenance of the project and describe how project-specific strategies were considered.

#### DOCUMENTATION TO SUBMIT TO USGBC
- Climate and natural hazard assessment report
- Documentation of identified observed, projected, and future natural hazards
- Documentation of two (or more) priority hazards selected
- For each priority hazard: IPCC emissions scenario, projected service life, hazard level, hazard risk rating, exposure, sensitivity, adaptive capacity, vulnerability, and overall risk levels
- Potential impact analysis (on project site/building function and during construction)
- Description of how assessment informed planning, design, and O&M strategies

#### CALCULATIONS REQUIRED
- None explicitly defined (assessment-based)

### IPp2: Human Impact Assessment

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Credit ID** | IPp2 |
| **Full Name** | Human Impact Assessment |
| **Type** | Prerequisite (Required) |
| **Points** | N/A |
| **New Construction** | Required |
| **Core and Shell** | Required |
| **Impact Area Alignment** | Quality of Life |

#### INTENT
To ensure that project development is guided by a thorough understanding of the social context of the local community, workforce, and supply chain, and to address potential social inequities.

#### REQUIREMENTS

**Achievement Pathways: New Construction and Core and Shell**

**Human Impact Assessment**

Complete a human impact assessment that draws on relevant information from the following **four specified categories**, as applicable:

**Category 1: Demographics**
This may include:
- Race and ethnicity
- Gender
- Age
- Income
- Employment rate
- Population density
- Education levels
- Household types
- Identification of nearby vulnerable populations

**Category 2: Local Infrastructure and Land Use**
This may include:
- Adjacent transportation and pedestrian infrastructure
- Adjacent diverse uses
- Relevant local or regional sustainability goals/commitments
- Applicable accessibility codes

**Category 3: Human Use and Health Impacts**
This may include:
- Housing affordability and availability
- Access to social services (e.g., healthcare, education, and social support networks)
- Community safety and local community groups
- Supply chain and construction workforce protections

**Category 4: Occupant Experience**
This may include:
- An opportunity for daylight, views, and operable windows
- Environmental conditions of air and water
- Adjacent soundscapes, lighting, and wind patterns within the context of the surrounding buildings (e.g., a microclimate, a solar scape, neighboring structures)

**Category 5: Other**
- Other relevant factors (specify)

**Follow-Up Requirement:**
Where possible, use the information from the assessment to inform the planning, design, and operations and maintenance of the project and describe how project-specific strategies were considered.

#### DOCUMENTATION TO SUBMIT TO USGBC
- Human impact assessment report
- Documentation covering all four specified categories (Demographics, Local Infrastructure and Land Use, Human Use and Health Impacts, Occupant Experience)
- Any additional "Other" category items specified
- Description of how assessment informed planning, design, and O&M strategies

#### CALCULATIONS REQUIRED
- None explicitly defined (assessment-based)

### IPp3: Carbon Assessment

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Credit ID** | IPp3 |
| **Full Name** | Carbon Assessment |
| **Type** | Prerequisite (Required) |
| **Points** | N/A |
| **New Construction** | Required |
| **Core and Shell** | Required |
| **Impact Area Alignment** | Decarbonization |

#### INTENT
To understand and reduce long-term direct and indirect carbon emissions, including on-site combustion, grid-supplied electricity, refrigerants, and embodied carbon.

#### REQUIREMENTS

**Achievement Pathways: New Construction and Core and Shell**

**Carbon Assessment**

USGBC will provide the project team with a **25-year projection** of the project's emissions from operations, refrigerants, and embodied carbon. The assessment will use data from the following credits/prerequisites:

| Data Source | Credit/Prerequisite |
|---|---|
| EAp1 | Operational Carbon Projection and Decarbonization Plan |
| EAp5 | Fundamental Refrigerant Management |
| MRp2 | Quantify and Assess Embodied Carbon |
| LTc4 | Transportation Demand Management (optional) |

#### DOCUMENTATION TO SUBMIT TO USGBC
- USGBC provides the 25-year projection (project team submits data from the prerequisite credits listed above)
- Operational carbon projection (from EAp1)
- Refrigerant management data (from EAp5)
- Embodied carbon assessment data (from MRp2)
- Transportation demand management data (from LTc4, optional)

#### CALCULATIONS REQUIRED
- 25-year projection of emissions (performed by USGBC using submitted data)
- Emissions from operations, refrigerants, and embodied carbon
- Optional: Transportation emissions

#### DATA TABLES / THRESHOLDS / METRICS
- **Projection Period:** 25 years
- **Emission Categories:** Operations, refrigerants, embodied carbon
- **Optional Addition:** Transportation demand management data

### IPp4: Tenant Guidelines

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Credit ID** | IPp4 |
| **Full Name** | Tenant Guidelines |
| **Type** | Prerequisite (Required) |
| **Points** | N/A |
| **New Construction** | Not Applicable |
| **Core and Shell** | Required |
| **Impact Area Alignment** | Decarbonization, Quality of Life, Ecological Conservation and Restoration |

#### INTENT
To communicate and coordinate the sustainable design and construction features of a base building with the tenants and facilitate tenant LEED certification so that the completed space more comprehensively addresses the rating system requirements.

#### REQUIREMENTS

**Achievement Pathways: Core and Shell only**

**Tenant Guidelines**

Create tenant guidelines to be shared with **all tenants before signing the lease**, including the following required content:

**Required Content Item 1:**
A description of the sustainable design and construction features incorporated in the Core and Shell project and the project's sustainability goals and objectives, including those for tenant spaces.

**Required Content Item 2:**
Guidance and recommendations for incorporating sustainable strategies, products, materials, and services in the tenant spaces. The guidelines must **refer to the attempted LEED prerequisites and credits of the base building** for content requirements.

**Required Content Item 3:**
A point of contact, from the base building team or new owner, for further coordination of base building design and construction documentation.

#### DOCUMENTATION TO SUBMIT TO USGBC
- Tenant guidelines document
- Confirmation that guidelines will be shared with all tenants before lease signing
- Description of sustainable design and construction features
- Guidance for tenant spaces referencing attempted LEED prerequisites and credits
- Point of contact information

#### CALCULATIONS REQUIRED
- None

### IPc1: Integrative Design Process

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Credit ID** | IPc1 |
| **Full Name** | Integrative Design Process |
| **Type** | Credit |
| **Points - New Construction** | 1 point |
| **Points - Core and Shell** | 1 point |
| **New Construction** | Available (1 point) |
| **Core and Shell** | Available (1 point) |
| **Impact Area Alignment** | Decarbonization, Quality of Life, Ecological Conservation and Restoration |

#### INTENT
To support high-performance, cost-effective, and cross-functional project outcomes through an early analysis and planning of the interrelationships among systems. To provide a holistic framework for project teams to collaboratively address decarbonization, quality of life, and ecological conservation and restoration across the entire LEED rating system.

#### REQUIREMENTS

**Achievement Pathways: New Construction and Core and Shell**

**Integrative Design Process (1 point)**

Beginning in **predesign** and continuing throughout **early occupancy**, identify and apply opportunities to achieve synergies across disciplines and building systems through the following initiatives:

**Initiative 1: Integrated Team**
- Assemble and convene an interdisciplinary project team with diverse perspectives
- Ensure the process is an equitable team effort through organized facilitation

**Initiative 2: Design Charette**
- During predesign or early in design, conduct a charette with the owner or owner's representative
- Participants must represent **at least four key perspectives**, such as:
  - Architect
  - Contractor
  - Energy modeler
  - Community engagement representatives
  - (Other relevant disciplines as applicable)

**Initiative 3: LEED Goal Setting**
- Work as a team to define a set of **specific and measurable project goals**
- Goals must address the LEED v5 impact areas of:
  - Decarbonization
  - Quality of life
  - Ecological conservation and restoration
- Incorporate these goals into the **owner's project requirements**

#### DOCUMENTATION TO SUBMIT TO USGBC
- Documentation of integrated team assembly (list of interdisciplinary team members showing diverse perspectives)
- Evidence of organized facilitation process
- Documentation of design charette (date, participants, owner/owner's rep presence)
- Confirmation that at least four key perspectives were represented at the charette
- Specific and measurable project goals addressing all three LEED v5 impact areas (decarbonization, quality of life, ecological conservation and restoration)
- Evidence that goals were incorporated into the owner's project requirements
- Timeline showing process began in predesign and continued through early occupancy

#### CALCULATIONS REQUIRED
- None explicitly defined (process-based credit)

### IPc2: Green Leases

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Credit ID** | IPc2 |
| **Full Name** | Green Leases |
| **Type** | Credit |
| **Points - New Construction** | Not Applicable |
| **Points - Core and Shell** | 1-6 points |
| **New Construction** | Not Available |
| **Core and Shell** | Available (1-6 points) |
| **Impact Area Alignment** | Decarbonization, Quality of Life, Ecological Conservation and Restoration |

#### INTENT
To ensure the tenants complete the sustainable design and construction features started by the base building so that the completed space comprehensively addresses the rating system requirements.

#### REQUIREMENTS

**Achievement Pathways: Core and Shell only**

Comply with a **combination** of the following options for a **maximum of 6 points**:

- **Option 1:** Standard Green Lease (1-6 points)
- **AND/OR**
- **Option 2:** Executed Standard Green Lease (+1 additional point)
- **AND/OR**
- **Option 3:** Green Lease Leaders Recognition (1-6 points)

---

##### OPTION 1: Standard Green Lease (1-6 points)

Develop a standard green lease document that establishes tenant fit-out standards and incorporates tenant requirements, as described below. Commit to incorporating the green lease documentation in each future tenant lease.

**For Owner-Fit-Out Spaces:**
For any spaces or systems intended to be fit-out by the project owner, identify the standards for the future fit-out, incorporate the criteria referenced in the standard green lease document, and commit to performing the fit-out(s) in accordance with these standards.

**Required Tenant Prerequisites:**
The tenant requirements shall include standards to ensure compliance with the following prerequisites for any tenant-installed systems:

| Prerequisite ID | Prerequisite Name |
|---|---|
| IPp1 | Climate Resilience Assessment |
| IPp2 | Human Impact Assessment |
| IPp3 | Carbon Assessment |
| WEp2 | Minimum Water Efficiency |
| EAp2 | Minimum Energy Efficiency |
| EAp3 | Fundamental Commissioning |
| EAp4 | Energy Metering and Reporting |
| MRp1 | Planning for Zero Waste Operations |
| EQp2 | Fundamental Air Quality |

**Best Practices for Points (Table 1):**

Points are earned by incorporating a combination of listed best practices into the green lease. The following best practices are available:

**Energy Best Practices:**
1. Require tenant to pay for their electric and nonelectric energy and water use
2. Implement a cost-recovery clause for energy-efficiency upgrades benefiting the tenant
3. Disclose tracked common area energy use, peak demand, peak thermal demand, and on-site combustion emissions to tenants
4. Disclose whole-building ENERGY STAR or locally applicable equivalent score to tenants
5. Implement energy management best practices
6. Ensure brokers or leasing agents have energy training
7. Request annual tenant energy disclosure
8. Require energy efficiency fit-out for tenants that improves upon EAp2: Minimum Energy Efficiency requirement
9. Establish a tenant energy efficiency engagement and training plan
10. Meter or submeter additional tenant energy use beyond that required in EAp4: Energy Metering and Reporting
11. Limit on-site combustion emissions

**Water Best Practices:**
12. Disclose tracked common area water use to tenants
13. Require water efficiency fit-out for tenants that improves upon WEp2: Minimum Water Efficiency
14. Meter or submeter additional tenant water use beyond that required in WEp1: Water Metering and Reporting
15. Implement water management best practices

**Indoor Air Quality Best Practices:**
16. Implement indoor air quality best practices

**Thermal Comfort Best Practices:**
17. Implement thermal comfort best practices

**Innovation:**
18. Demonstrate innovation in leasing

**Points Scoring Table (Table 1):**

| Number of Additional Best Practices Incorporated | Points Earned |
|---|---|
| Fewer than 3 | 1 |
| 4-6 | 2 |
| 7-9 | 3 |
| 10-12 | 4 |
| 13-15 | 5 |
| 16 or more | 6 |

---

##### OPTION 2: Executed Standard Green Lease (1 additional point)

One additional point is awarded by providing documentation of an **executed tenant green lease** or **tenant letter of attestation** meeting the criteria in Option 1.

This point is **additive** to points earned under Option 1 (for a maximum total of 7 points if combined with Option 1 maximum).

---

##### OPTION 3: Green Lease Leaders Recognition (1-6 points)

Earn Green Lease Leaders recognition:

| Recognition Level | Points |
|---|---|
| Silver level | 1 point |
| Gold level | 3 points |
| Platinum level | 6 points |

**Note:** The scoring appears to be based on earning recognition through the Green Lease Leaders program (external to LEED). The project can achieve 1, 3, or 6 points based on the level of recognition earned.

---

#### SUMMARY: MAXIMUM POINTS ACHIEVABLE

| Combination | Maximum Points |
|---|---|
| Option 1 only | 6 points |
| Option 1 + Option 2 | 7 points (6 + 1) |
| Option 3 only | 6 points |
| Mixed combinations | Maximum 6 points total per credit description |

**Note:** The credit states "Comply with a combination of the following options for a maximum of 6 points." The Option 2 additional point may be additive beyond this 6-point maximum, bringing the total to 7.

#### DOCUMENTATION TO SUBMIT TO USGBC

**For Option 1:**
- Standard green lease document
- Documentation showing tenant fit-out standards
- Documentation showing incorporation of all required prerequisites (IPp1, IPp2, IPp3, WEp2, EAp2, EAp3, EAp4, MRp1, EQp2)
- List of all additional best practices incorporated into the lease
- Commitment documentation for incorporating green lease in each future tenant lease
- For owner-fit-out spaces: standards documentation and commitment to perform fit-outs accordingly

**For Option 2:**
- Executed tenant green lease OR tenant letter of attestation
- Documentation showing the executed lease/attestation meets Option 1 criteria

**For Option 3:**
- Green Lease Leaders recognition certificate or documentation at Silver, Gold, or Platinum level

#### CALCULATIONS REQUIRED
- Count of additional best practices incorporated (to determine points under Option 1, Table 1)

#### DATA TABLES / THRESHOLDS / METRICS

**Best Practices Scoring Thresholds:**
- < 3 practices = 1 point
- 4-6 practices = 2 points
- 7-9 practices = 3 points
- 10-12 practices = 4 points
- 13-15 practices = 5 points
- 16+ practices = 6 points

**Total Available Best Practices:** At least 18 best practices listed across energy, water, indoor air quality, thermal comfort, and innovation categories.

# EQp1: Construction Management

## Credit Information
- **ID:** EQp1
- **Name:** Construction Management
- **Type:** Prerequisite (Required)
- **Applicability:** New Construction and Core and Shell
- **Points:** N/A (Required)

## Intent
To promote the well-being of construction workers and building occupants by minimizing environmental quality problems associated with construction and renovation.

## Requirements

### Achievement Pathway
**New Construction and Core and Shell**

Develop and implement construction management practices for the construction and preoccupancy phases of the building. The practices must address all of the following:

### 1. No Smoking
- Prohibit smoking during construction except in designated smoking areas located at least 25 feet (7.5 meters) from the building.
- Install signage that prohibits smoking during construction.

### 2. Extreme Heat Protection
- Implement measures that protect construction workers from extreme heat.

### 3. HVAC Protection
- Keep contaminants out of the HVAC system.
- Do not run permanently installed equipment, if possible, but if it must be used, maintain proper filtration.
- Replace all air filtration media after completion of construction and before occupancy.
- Confirm that testing and balance work is completed with new filtration.

### 4. Source Control
- Keep sources of contaminants out of the building and have a plan to eliminate any that are introduced.
- Store carpets, acoustic ceiling panels, fabric wall coverings, insulation, upholstery and furnishings, and other absorptive materials in a designated area protected from moisture damage.

### 5. Pathway Interruption
- Prevent circulation of contaminated air when cutting concrete or wood, sanding drywall, installing volatile-organic-compound-emitting materials, or performing other activities that affect indoor air quality in other workspaces.
- Isolate areas of work to prevent contamination of other spaces, whether they are finished or not. Seal doorways and windows, or tent off areas as needed using temporary barriers.
- Use walk-off mats at entryways to reduce introduced dirt and pollutants.
- Use dust guards and collectors on saws and other tools.

### 6. Housekeeping
- Maintain a clean jobsite.
- Use vacuum cleaners with high-efficiency particulate filters.
- Use sweeping compounds or wetting agents for dust control when sweeping.

### 7. Scheduling
- Sequence construction activities to reduce air quality problems.
- For renovation projects, coordinate construction activities to minimize or eliminate disruption of operations in occupied areas.

## Impact Area Alignment
- Decarbonization: —
- Quality of Life: ✓
- Ecological Conservation and Restoration: —

## Key Standards Referenced
- None specifically (general best practices)

## Documentation to Submit to USGBC
- Construction management plan documenting all seven required practices
- Evidence of signage installation for no-smoking policy
- Documentation of extreme heat protection measures
- Filtration media replacement records
- Photos/documentation of source control, pathway interruption, housekeeping, and scheduling measures

## Calculations Required
- Distance verification: Smoking areas must be ≥ 25 feet (7.5 m) from building

# EQp2: Fundamental Air Quality

## Credit Information
- **ID:** EQp2
- **Name:** Fundamental Air Quality
- **Type:** Prerequisite (Required)
- **Applicability:** New Construction and Core and Shell
- **Points:** N/A (Required)

## Intent
To design for above-average indoor air quality to support occupant health and well-being.

## Requirements

### Achievement Pathways
**New Construction and Core and Shell**

Must comply with ALL three:
1. Investigate Regional and Local Air Quality
2. Ventilation and Filtration Design
3. Entryway System Design

---

### Requirement 1: Investigate Regional and Local Air Quality

Investigate outdoor air quality in accordance with **ASHRAE Standard 62.1-2022, Sections 4.1–4.3**.

---

### Requirement 2: Ventilation and Filtration Design

Meet the requirements of **ASHRAE Standard 62.1-2022, Sections 5 and 6**. Use the ventilation rate procedure (VRP), the IAQ procedure (IAQP), the natural ventilation procedure, or a combination thereof.

Where the VRP or IAQP are used, comply with the following additional provisions:

#### Filtration
Each central HVAC system that supplies outdoor air and/or recirculated air to regularly occupied spaces must meet one of the following:
- **MERV 13**, in accordance with **ASHRAE Standard 52.2-2017**; OR
- **Equivalent filtration media class of ePM1 50%**, as defined by **ISO 16890-2016**, Particulate Air Filters for General Ventilation — Determination of the Filtration Performance; OR
- **In-room air-cleaning systems**:
  - Systems tested for effectiveness and safety per **ASHRAE Standard 241-2023, Section 7.4 (and Normative Appendix A)**
  - If treating for particles and gases, use systems tested for effectiveness per **ASHRAE 62.1-2022, Addendum N**
  - If treating for infectious aerosols, use systems tested for effectiveness per **ASHRAE Standard 241-2023, Section 7**

#### Outdoor Air Measurement
- Provide outdoor airflow measurement devices for all mechanical ventilation systems with outdoor air intake flow greater than **1,000 cfm (472 L/s)**.

#### Healthcare Spaces
- For healthcare spaces, meet the requirements of **Sections 6–10 of ASHRAE Standard 170-2021**.

#### Residential: Dwelling Unit Provisions
If the project building contains residential units, each dwelling unit must meet all the following:

- **Mechanical Ventilation**: Design and install a dwelling-unit mechanical ventilation system that complies with **ASHRAE 62.2-2022, Sections 4, 6.6, and 6.7**. Supply and balanced mechanical ventilation systems must be designed and constructed to provide ventilation air directly from the outdoors. Mechanical ventilation systems are not required when the project meets the exception in **ASHRAE 62.2-2022, Section 4.1.1**.

- **Local Mechanical Exhaust**: Design and install local mechanical exhaust systems in each kitchen and bathroom (including half baths) that comply with **ASHRAE 62.2-2022, Sections 5 and 7**. Exhaust air to the outdoors. Do not route exhaust ducts to terminate in attics or interstitial spaces. Recirculating range hoods or recirculating over-the-range microwaves do NOT satisfy kitchen exhaust requirements.
  - For exhaust hood systems capable of exhausting in excess of **400 cfm (188 L/s)**, provide makeup air at a rate approximately equal to the exhaust air rate. Makeup air systems must have a means of closure and be automatically controlled to start and operate simultaneously with the exhaust system.
  - Use **ENERGY STAR labeled bathroom exhaust fans** in all bathrooms (including half baths) or performance equivalent for projects outside the U.S.
  - An HRV or ERV may be used to exhaust single or multiple bathrooms if it has an efficacy level meeting the **ENERGY STAR Technical Specifications for Residential Heat-Recovery Ventilators and Energy-Recovery Ventilators (H/ERVs), version 2.3** as certified by the Home Ventilating Institute.

- **Unvented Combustion Appliances**: Unvented combustion appliances (ovens and ranges excluded) are NOT allowed.

- **CO Monitors**: A carbon monoxide (CO) monitor must be installed on each floor of each dwelling unit, hard-wired with a battery backup. CO monitors are required in all types of units, regardless of the type of equipment installed.

- **Fireplaces and Woodstoves**: Must have solid glass enclosures or doors that seal when closed. Any indoor fireplaces and woodstoves that are not closed combustion or power-vented must pass a backdraft potential test to ensure depressurization of the combustion appliance zone is **less than 5 Pa**.

- **Space- and Water-Heating Equipment**: Must be designed and installed with closed combustion (sealed supply air and exhaust ducting), or with power-vented exhaust, or located in a detached utility building or open-air facility.

---

### Requirement 3: Entryway System Design

Install permanent entryway systems to capture dirt and particulates entering the building at primary exterior entrances. **There is no length requirement** for entryway systems.

## Impact Area Alignment

- Quality of Life: ✓
- Ecological Conservation and Restoration: —

- ASHRAE Standard 52.2-2017

- ASHRAE Standard 241-2023

# EQp3: No Smoking or Vehicle Idling

## Credit Information
- **ID:** EQp3
- **Name:** No Smoking or Vehicle Idling
- **Type:** Prerequisite (Required)
- **Applicability:** New Construction and Core and Shell
- **Points:** N/A (Required)

## Intent
To minimize exposure to tobacco smoke, smoke from tobacco substitutes or cannabis, and vehicle emissions.

## Requirements

### Achievement Pathways
**New Construction and Core and Shell**

Must comply with ALL of the following:
1. Prohibit Smoking
2. Prohibit Vehicle Idling

---

### Prohibit Smoking

- **Indoor Smoking**: Prohibit smoking inside the building with limited exceptions.
- **Outdoor Smoking**: Prohibit smoking outside the building except in designated smoking areas located at least **25 feet (7.5 meters)** (or the maximum extent allowable by local codes) from all entries, outdoor air intakes, and operable windows.
- **School Projects**: Prohibit all smoking on-site.

### Prohibit Vehicle Idling
- Prohibit vehicle idling on-site.

### Communication and Enforcement
- Communicate the no-smoking and vehicle idling prohibition policy to occupants.
- Have in place provisions for enforcement or prohibitive signage.

---

### Residential Additional Requirements

- Meet the preceding requirements for all areas inside and outside the building **except dwelling units and private balconies**.

#### For Residential Projects That Do NOT Prohibit Smoking
Each dwelling unit where smoking will be permitted must meet the following compartmentalization requirements:

- Perform a **blower door test** of residential dwelling units, following procedures in **ANSI/RESNET/ICC 380** or equivalent.
- For each unit tested, demonstrate a maximum leakage of enclosure area that is **no more than 1.5 times** the thresholds in Table 1 ("enclosure area" = all surfaces enclosing the dwelling unit, including exterior and party walls, floors, and ceilings).
- Demonstrate a **weighted average leakage** of enclosure area for the building, including dwelling units, that complies with the caps in Table 1.

**Table 1. Caps on Air Leakage Rates**

| Building Conditioned Floor Area (CFA) | Pressure Test Conditions | Maximum Air Leakage - New Construction | Maximum Air Leakage - Major Renovation |
|---|---|---|---|
| ≥ 5,000 sq. ft. (465 sq. m.) | At 50 Pa (0.2 in H₂O) | 0.13 cfm/sq. ft. (0.65 L/s·sq. m.) | 0.20 cfm/sq. ft. (1.0 L/s·sq. m.) |
| ≥ 5,000 sq. ft. (465 sq. m.) | At 75 Pa (0.3 in H₂O) | 0.18 cfm/sq. ft. (0.90 L/s·sq. m.) | 0.27 cfm/sq. ft. (1.35 L/s·sq. m.) |
| < 5,000 sq. ft. (465 sq. m.) | At 50 Pa (0.2 in H₂O) | 1 ACH | 1.5 ACH |
| < 5,000 sq. ft. (465 sq. m.) | At 75 Pa (0.3 in H₂O) | 1.35 ACH | 2 ACH |

*Note: cfm = cubic feet per minute; L/s = liters per second; ACH = air changes per hour*

## Impact Area Alignment
- Decarbonization: —
- Quality of Life: ✓
- Ecological Conservation and Restoration: ✓

## Key Standards Referenced
- ANSI/RESNET/ICC 380 (blower door testing)

## Documentation to Submit to USGBC
- No-smoking and no-idling policy documentation
- Site plan showing designated smoking areas ≥ 25 ft from entries/intakes/windows
- Signage documentation
- For residential projects not prohibiting smoking: Blower door test reports with enclosure area leakage calculations per Table 1

## Calculations Required
- Distance verification for smoking areas
- For residential: Enclosure area calculations, blower door test results, weighted average leakage calculations

# EQc1: Enhanced Air Quality

## Credit Information
- **ID:** EQc1
- **Name:** Enhanced Air Quality
- **Type:** Credit
- **Applicability:** New Construction (1 point) and Core and Shell (1 point)
- **Points:** 1 point
- **Exemplary Performance:** Available (30% increased ventilation for 1 additional point via Innovation)

## Intent
To design for increased indoor air quality (IAQ) to better protect the health of building occupants.

## Requirements

### Achievement Pathways
**New Construction and Core and Shell**

Design the building to exceed the requirements of **ASHRAE 62.1-2022, Section 6**.

- If using the **VRP** to comply with EQp2: Fundamental Air Quality, use **Option 1 or Option 2**
- If using the **IAQP**, use **Option 2 only**

---

### Option 1: Increased Ventilation (1 point)

Increase breathing zone outdoor air ventilation rates by at least **15% above the minimum rates** (or **30% for exemplary performance**) as determined in EQp2: Fundamental Air Quality.

- Increased outdoor air rates should be provided to **95% of all regularly occupied spaces**.

---

### Option 2: Enhanced Indoor Air Quality Design (1 point)

In addition to the design compounds and design limits outlined in **ASHRAE 62.1-2022, Tables 6-5 and 6-6**, design and verify enhanced IAQ using the lower design limits in Table 1 below.

**Table 1. Additional Design Limits for Enhanced IAQ Design**

| Design Compound or PM2.5 | Enhanced IAQP Design Limit |
|---|---|
| PM2.5 | 10 µg/m³ |
| Formaldehyde | 20 µg/m³ |
| Ozone | 10 ppb |

*Note: ppb = parts per billion*

## Impact Area Alignment
- Decarbonization: —
- Quality of Life: ✓
- Ecological Conservation and Restoration: —

## Key Standards Referenced
- ASHRAE 62.1-2022 (Section 6, Tables 6-5, 6-6)

## Documentation to Submit to USGBC

**For Option 1:**
- Ventilation calculations showing ≥ 15% (or 30% for EP) increase above ASHRAE 62.1 minimums
- Floor plans or schedule showing 95% of regularly occupied spaces receive increased ventilation

**For Option 2:**
- IAQ design documentation showing compliance with enhanced design limits in Table 1
- Documentation of design compounds and verification method
- Test reports or modeling results demonstrating compliance with PM2.5, Formaldehyde, and Ozone limits

## Calculations Required
- Option 1: Ventilation rate increase calculation (minimum 15% above ASHRAE 62.1 VRP)
- Option 2: IAQ modeling/calculations showing compliance with enhanced design limits

# EQc2: Occupant Experience

## Credit Information
- **ID:** EQc2
- **Name:** Occupant Experience
- **Type:** Credit
- **Applicability:**
  - New Construction: 1–7 points
  - Core and Shell: 1–7 points
- **Points:** Up to 7 points

## Intent
To move beyond neutral or sufficient spaces toward human-centered design that supports customization, enjoyment, and emotional connections between people and the building, thus increasing the likelihood of consistent satisfaction and ongoing stewardship.

## Requirements: NEW CONSTRUCTION

### Achievement Pathways (New Construction)

| Option | Path | Points |
|--------|------|--------|
| Option 1. Biophilic Environment | Path 1: Integrated Biophilic Design | 1 |
| | Path 2: Quality Views | 2–3 |
| Option 2. Adaptable Environment | | 1 |
| Option 3. Thermal Environment | | 1 |
| Option 4. Sound Environment | Path 1: Mapping Acoustic Expectations | 1 |
| | Path 2: Acoustic Criteria | 2 |
| Option 5. Lighting Environment | Path 1: Solar Glare | 1 |
| | Path 2: Quality Electric Lighting | 1 |
| | Path 3: Proximity to Windows for Daylight Access | 1 |
| | Path 4: Daylight Simulation | 1–4 |

**Maximum: 7 points**

---

### Option 1. Biophilic Environment (1–4 points)

#### PATH 1. Integrated Biophilic Design (1 point)
Integrate biophilic design that demonstrates each of the following five principles adapted from *The Practice of Biophilic Design* by Kellert and Calabrese:

1. Biophilic design requires repeated and sustained engagement with nature.
2. Biophilic design focuses on human adaptations to the natural world that, over evolutionary time, have advanced people's health, fitness, and well-being.
3. Biophilic design encourages an emotional attachment to the building and building location.
4. Biophilic design promotes positive interactions between people and nature that encourage an expanded sense of relationship and responsibility for the human and natural communities.
5. Biophilic design encourages mutual reinforcing, interconnected, and integrated architectural solutions.

#### PATH 2. Quality Views (2–3 points)
Provide occupants with a view to the outdoor natural or urban environment for:
- **75%** of regularly occupied floor area = **2 points**
- **90%** of regularly occupied floor area = **3 points**

Exclusions: Auditoriums, conference rooms dedicated to video conferencing, and gymnasiums may be excluded. Views into interior atria may be used to meet up to **30%** of the required area.

**View Requirements:**
- Views must be through glass with **visible light transmittance (VLT) above 40%**
- If glazing has frits, patterns, or tints, the view must be preserved (neutral gray, bronze, blue-green tints acceptable)
- Views must include at least one of the following:
  - Nature, urban landmarks, or art; OR
  - Objects at least **25 feet (7.5 meters)** from the exterior of the glazing
- Occupants must have **direct access to the view** and be within **three times the head height** of the glazing

---

### Option 2. Adaptable Environment (1 point)

Allow occupants choice and flexibility and/or the capability to adapt the space to meet their individual needs. Provide variability and/or optionality for thermal, sound, and lighting environments that invite occupants to either alter their experience and/or move between sensory zones.

- Include at least **one accessible quiet space** that allows occupants to retreat from high levels of sensory stimulation.
- Demonstrate at least one of the following additional strategies:

**Additional Strategies:**
- Provide socializing, meeting, dining, and/or working areas where occupants can sit outside the main action and have permanent architectural features at their backs that create a comfortable, semi-protected space overlooking the larger area (prospect). Provide alternative paths around the perimeter.
- Provide choice in furniture configuration and a variety of seating to accommodate a wide range of body types, including seating with back rests and without arm rests.
- Provide height variety for permanently installed fixtures (counters, sinks) and/or height-adjustable tables and desks.
- Provide outdoor or transitional space encouraging interaction with nature that is flexible or multiuse. Ensure space is easily accessible for all occupants from within the building or located within **200 feet (60 meters)** of a building entrance or access point.

---

### Option 3. Thermal Environment (1 point)

Design indoor occupied spaces to meet the requirements of **ASHRAE Standard 55-2023, Thermal Environmental Conditions for Human Occupancy** (with errata).

Investigate thermal conditions and explain how the design considers:
- Thermal conditions that align and adjust with changing seasons
- Overcooling during nontemperate seasons
  - Design solutions for newly arrived occupants or those transitioning between different thermal environments
  - Design solutions for long-term occupants in transition spaces to customize their working area
  - Support for occupants carrying out different tasks requiring varying levels of movement
  - Cooling solutions for those completing high-movement tasks

---

### Option 4. Sound Environment (1–2 points)

#### PATH 1. Mapping Acoustic Expectations for Indoor and Outdoor Spaces (1 point)

Determine the desired sound environment early in the design process by mapping the acoustic expectations for each primary indoor and outdoor space. Categories to consider:

**Classifications:**
- **Noise exposure zones**: High risk, medium risk, low risk, or no risk
- **Acoustic comfort**: Loud zone, quiet zone, mixed zone, circulation, sensitive, or no specific expectations
- **Acoustic privacy**: High speech security, confidential speech privacy, normal speech privacy, marginal speech privacy, or no privacy
- **Communication zones**: Excellent, good, marginal, none or no specific expectations
- **Soundscape management**: Preserve, improve, restore, mitigate, specialize (e.g., for wellness, therapeutic, or agency in equity), or no specific expectations

Define acoustic criteria and potential design strategies and solutions. Categories to consider include:
- Internally generated background noise
- Externally intrusive background noise
- Electronically generated masking sound
- Outdoor acoustic environment
- Airborne sound reverberation
- Sound insulation
- Vibration insulation
- Impact noise

#### PATH 2. Acoustic Criteria for Indoor and Outdoor Spaces (2 points)

Through calculations, modeling, and/or measurements, demonstrate that the mapping exercise completed in Path 1 informed design strategies and solutions to meet acoustic criteria for at least **75% of the occupied spaces** and **all classrooms and other core learning spaces**.

---

### Option 5. Lighting Environment (1–6 points)

#### PATH 1. Solar Glare (1 point)
Provide manual or automatic (with manual override) glare-control devices in all regularly occupied spaces that will receive direct or reflected sun penetration. Spaces designed intentionally for direct sunlight may be excluded.

#### PATH 2. Quality Electric Lighting (1 point)

**Electric Light Glare Control** — Each luminaire shall meet one of:
- Calculated luminance of **less than 6,000 cd/m²** between 45 and 90 degrees from nadir; OR
- **UGR of 19 or lower** using the UGR tabular method for each space; OR
- **UGR of 19 or lower** using software modeling calculations (per NEMA White Paper on Unified Glare Rating)

**Color Rendering** — Use luminaires with:
- **CRI of at least 90**; OR
- Meet IES TM-30-20 requirements in Table below:

| Measure | Requirement |
|---------|-------------|
| Fidelity index Rf | 78 or higher |
| Gamut index Rg | 95 or higher |
| Red Local Chroma Shift Rcs,h1 | −1% to 15% |

#### PATH 3. Proximity to Windows for Daylight Access (1 point)
Design interior layout so at least **30% of regularly occupied area** is within a **20-foot (6-meter)** horizontal distance of envelope glazing with **VLT above 40%**. Exclude areas with visual obstructions incapable of providing a view to envelope glazing.

#### PATH 4. Daylight Simulation (1–4 points)

Perform daylight simulation analysis using **IES LM-83-23** protocols:

- Calculate **sDA 300/50%** and **ASE 1000,250** for each regularly occupied space
- **sDA 150/50%** may be used for areas without visual tasks (design targets of 225 lux)
- For spaces with ASE net(1000,250h) > 20%, identify glare control measures
- Calculate average sDA for total regularly occupied floor area
- Do NOT exclude spaces based on ASE

**Table 2. Points for Daylight Simulation (New Construction)**

| Average sDA 300/50% or sDA 150/50% Value | Points |
|---|---|
| ≥ 40% | 1 |
| ≥ 55% | 2 |
| ≥ 65% | 3 |
| ≥ 75% | 4 |

# EQc3: Accessibility and Inclusion

## Credit Information
- **ID:** EQc3
- **Name:** Accessibility and Inclusion
- **Type:** Credit
- **Applicability:** New Construction (1 point) and Core and Shell (1 point)
- **Points:** 1 point

## Intent
To support the diverse needs of occupants and increase widespread usability of the building to foster an individual and collective sense of belonging.

## Requirements

### Achievement Pathways
**New Construction and Core and Shell**

**Prerequisite Step: Comply With Local Accessibility Codes**
All projects must support access for those with physical disabilities through designs meeting all locally applicable accessibility codes identified in **IPp2: Human Impact Assessment**.

If there is **no code in place**, include the following strategies:
- Accessible routes or regularly used exterior building entrances have ramps to accommodate elevation change
- All doors for human passage have minimum clear width of **32 inches (0.86 meters)**
- Reception desks, security counters, and service counters all have a front approach, wheelchair accessible section

**Credit Requirement:**
AND — Include at least **10 of the following** accessibility and inclusion strategies most relevant to the project that go **beyond** the locally applicable accessibility code:

---

### Accessibility for Physical Diversity
1. Provide wave-to-open or vertical hand/foot press door operators at all regularly used building entrances
2. Design meeting spaces to accommodate mobility devices for at least **10% of occupants**
3. Incorporate accessible and inclusive equipment and activities in fitness facilities (ensure open and accessible route to and around equipment)
4. Where inaccessible routes are provided (e.g., stairs), provide an alternate accessible route that starts and terminates at the same location

### Accessibility for Safety and Aging
5. Provide nonslip flooring
6. Fix underside of area rugs to floor and provide transition strips at all edges
7. Provide visual indication or railing at all full-height glazing (except private residences)
8. Provide audible and visual alerts for emergency alerts
9. Provide closed risers (visually and physically) in all stairwells
10. Use visual contrast between walls and floors, walls and doors, and walls and casework
11. Provide visual, tactile, contrasting, or photoluminescent warnings at floor-level changes

### Accessibility for Social Health
12. Provide lactation room pods
13. Provide at least one fully accessible, all-gender, single-use restroom OR one multiuse, all-gender restroom on each floor
14. Include at least one adult changing station or table in a designated, accessible restroom or family restroom, or in one men's and one women's restroom
15. Provide signage in all languages spoken by more than **5% of the local population**

# EQc4: Resilient Spaces

## Credit Information
- **ID:** EQc4
- **Name:** Resilient Spaces
- **Type:** Credit
- **Applicability:** New Construction (1–2 points) and Core and Shell (1–2 points)
- **Points:** Up to 2 points

## Intent
To support design features that increase the capacity for occupants to adapt to changing climate conditions and be protected from events that may compromise the quality of the indoor environment and subsequently occupant health and well-being.

## Requirements

### Achievement Pathways
**New Construction and Core and Shell**

| Option | Description | Points | Applicability |
|--------|-------------|--------|---------------|
| Option 1 | Management Mode for Episodic Outdoor Ambient Conditions | 1 | New Construction ONLY |
| Option 2 | Management Mode for Respiratory Diseases | 1 | New Construction ONLY |
| Option 3 | Design for Occupant Thermal Safety During Power Outages | 1–2 | All |
| Option 4 | Operable Windows | 1–2 | All |

**Maximum: 2 points**

---

### Option 1. Management Mode for Episodic Outdoor Ambient Conditions — New Construction Only (1 point)

Design systems with the capability to operate an **episodic outdoor event management mode** as described in **ASHRAE Guideline 44**. The mode should address varying outdoor conditions or events that could negatively influence IAQ, such as wildland fire smoke.

- Include the management mode in the design and commissioning documents
- Verify proper implementation of the mode during commissioning

---

### Option 2. Management Mode for Respiratory Diseases — New Construction Only (1 point)

Design occupied spaces with the capability to operate an **infection risk management mode** that provides the minimum equivalent clean airflow rates outlined in **ASHRAE 241-2023, Section 5.1**.

- Include the management mode in the design and commissioning documents as outlined in **ASHRAE 241-2023, Section B10.2 Design Documentation**
- Verify proper implementation of the mode during commissioning

---

### Option 3. Design for Occupant Thermal Safety During Power Outages (1–2 points)

#### PATH 1. Consider Extreme Heat (1 point)
Demonstrate through thermal modeling that the building will passively maintain thermally habitable conditions during a power outage lasting **two days** during peak summertime conditions of a **TMY (Typical Meteorological Year)**.

- Designate specific **thermal safety zones** where habitable conditions will be maintained during a power outage

#### PATH 2. Consider Extreme Cold (1 point)
Demonstrate through thermal modeling or **Passive House certification** that the building will passively maintain thermally habitable conditions during a power outage lasting **two days** during peak wintertime conditions of a **TMY**.

- Designate specific **thermal safety zones** where habitable conditions will be maintained during a power outage

---

### Option 4. Operable Windows (1–2 points)
Design the following percentage of regularly occupied spaces to have **operable windows** with capability to provide access to outdoor air during heat waves or localized power outages:

- **50%** of regularly occupied spaces = **1 point**
- **75%** of regularly occupied spaces = **2 points**

The windows must meet the opening size and location requirements of **ASHRAE 62.1-2022, Section 6.4**.

## Impact Area Alignment
- Decarbonization: —
- Quality of Life: ✓
- Ecological Conservation and Restoration: —

## Key Standards Referenced
- ASHRAE Guideline 44 (episodic outdoor event management mode)
- ASHRAE 241-2023 (infection risk management mode)
- TMY (Typical Meteorological Year) weather data
- Passive House certification (Path 2 alternative)
- ASHRAE 62.1-2022, Section 6.4 (operable windows)

## Documentation to Submit to USGBC

**Option 1:**
- Design documentation showing episodic outdoor event management mode per ASHRAE Guideline 44
- Commissioning verification report

**Option 2:**
- Design documentation showing infection risk management mode with equivalent clean airflow rates per ASHRAE 241-2023, Section 5.1
- ASHRAE 241-2023, Section B10.2 Design Documentation
- Commissioning verification report

**Option 3:**
- Thermal modeling report demonstrating passive habitable conditions during 2-day power outage (summer and/or winter TMY conditions)
- Identification of designated thermal safety zones on floor plans
- Passive House certification documentation (if using Path 2 alternative)

**Option 4:**
- Floor plans showing operable windows and % of regularly occupied spaces served
- Window schedule showing compliance with ASHRAE 62.1-2022, Section 6.4

## Calculations Required
- Option 3: Thermal modeling under TMY peak conditions with no power for 48 hours
- Option 4: % of regularly occupied spaces with ASHRAE 62.1-2022 Section 6.4 compliant operable windows

# EQc5: Air Quality Testing and Monitoring

## Credit Information
- **ID:** EQc5
- **Name:** Air Quality Testing and Monitoring
- **Type:** Credit
- **Applicability:** New Construction (1–2 points); **Not applicable to Core and Shell**
- **Points:** Up to 2 points

## Intent
To support better management of indoor air quality and identify opportunities for health-based approaches to building operations.

## Requirements

### Achievement Pathways (New Construction Only)

| Option | Path | Points |
|--------|------|--------|
| Option 1. Preoccupancy Air Testing | Path 1: Particulate Matter and Inorganic Gases | 1 |
| | Path 2: Volatile Organic Compounds | 1 |
| Option 2. Continuous Indoor Air Monitoring | | 1 |

**Maximum: 2 points**

---

### Option 1. Preoccupancy Air Testing (1–2 points)

After construction ends and before occupancy, but under **ventilation conditions typical for occupancy**, conduct baseline IAQ testing. Retail projects may conduct testing within **14 days of occupancy**.

**Number of measurements** per Table 1:

**Table 1. Number of Measurements Required for Preoccupancy Air Testing**

| Total Occupied Floor Area | Number of Measurements |
|---|---|
| ≤ 5,000 sq. ft. (500 sq. m.) | 1 |
| > 5,000 and ≤ 15,000 sq. ft. (1,500 sq. m.) | 2 |
| > 15,000 and ≤ 25,000 sq. ft. (2,500 sq. m.) | 3 |
| > 25,000 and ≤ 200,000 sq. ft. (20,000 sq. m.) | 4 + 1 per each 25,000 sq. ft. above 25,000 |
| > 200,000 sq. ft. | 10 + 1 per each 50,000 sq. ft. above 200,000 |

Measurements must be taken in **representative locations** of the building.

---

#### PATH 1. Particulate Matter and Inorganic Gases (1 point)

Test for PM and inorganic gases in Table 2 using an allowed test method. Demonstrate contaminants do not exceed concentration limits. Measure for a **four-hour period** and calculate **peak concentration for CO** and **average concentration for ozone, PM2.5, and PM10**.

**Table 2. Limits for PM and Inorganic Gases**

| Contaminant (CAS#) | Concentration Limit | Allowed Test Methods (Laboratory) | Direct Reading Instrument Minimum Specifications |
|---|---|---|---|
| Carbon monoxide (CO) | 9 ppm; no more than 2 ppm above outdoor levels | ISO 4224; EPA Compendium Method IP-3; GB/T 18883-2002 (China) | Direct calibrated electrochemical instrument: ±3% accuracy, 0.1 ppm resolution; OR NDIR CO sensors: 1% of 10 ppm full scale accuracy, <0.1 ppm display resolution |
| Particulates (attainment areas) | ISO class 8 or lower per ISO 14644-1:2015; OR PM10: 50 µg/m³; PM2.5: 12 µg/m³ | IP-10A | Accuracy (±): Greater of 5 µg/m³ or 20% of reading; Resolution (±): 5 µg/m³ |
| Particulates (nonattainment areas) | ISO class 8 or lower per ISO 14644-1:2015; OR PM10: 50 µg/m³; PM2.5: 35 µg/m³ | IP-10A | Accuracy (±): Greater of 5 µg/m³ or 20% of reading; Resolution (±): 5 µg/m³ |
| Ozone | 0.07 ppm; OR 0.01 ppm for projects pursuing EQc1 Enhanced Air Quality Option 1 Path 2 | ISO 13964; ASTM D5149-02; EPA designated methods | Accuracy: Greater of 5 ppb or 20% of reading; Resolution (5 min. average): ±5 ppb |

*Note: ppm = parts per million; ppb = parts per billion*

AND/OR perform a **screening test for TVOC**:
- Use **ISO 16000-6**, **EPA TO-17**, or **EPA TO-15** to collect and analyze air sample
- Calculate TVOC value per **EN 16516:2017**; **CDPH Standard Method v1.2 2017, Section 3.9.4**; or alternative with full method description
- If TVOC levels exceed **500 µg/m³**, investigate by comparing individual VOC levels from GC/MS results to health-based limits
- Correct any identified issues and retest if necessary

### Option 2. Continuous Indoor Air Monitoring (1 point)

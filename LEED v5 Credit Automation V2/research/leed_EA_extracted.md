<a name="eap1"></a>
## EAp1: OPERATIONAL CARBON PROJECTION AND DECARBONIZATION PLAN

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Credit ID** | EAp1 |
| **Type** | Prerequisite (Required) |
| **Applicability** | New Construction, Core and Shell |
| **Points** | N/A (Required) |
| **Impact Area** | Decarbonization |

### Intent
To enable building stakeholders to visualize how their current design decisions will impact their project's long-term operational carbon emissions and to ensure that stakeholders are planning for low-carbon outcomes from the project's inception.

### Achievement Pathways
**New Construction and Core and Shell**

Four required components must ALL be completed:

#### 1. Design Analysis
Analyze efficiency, peak load reduction, and decarbonization measures during the early stages of the design process and account for the results in design decision-making using at least one of the following methodologies:
- Simplified energy modeling
- Analysis from similar projects
- Analysis from published data

#### 2. Site Energy Estimate
Estimate the amount of each type of energy the project will use annually in terms of site energy and **submit the data to USGBC**.

#### 3. Review Carbon Projection
Using the annual energy use data submitted, the project's current grid data, and the project's location, **USGBC will generate** a business as usual (BAU) projection of the project's carbon emissions from energy use from the present through a **25-year period**.

**Projects subject to a carbon-based building performance standard (BPS)** must:
- Create an ordinance-specific BAU with a carbon projection based on the electrical coefficients as defined in the ordinance
- Overlay showing the caps applicable to the project
- Calculate the assessed annual fines or fees that will apply for exceeding the caps and the cumulative fines or fees over a 25-year period

The building owner or owner's representative shall **attest** that they have reviewed the BAU carbon projections and fee projection.

#### 4. Decarbonization Plan
**Path 1. Design for Electrification**
- Earn 4 or 5 points in EAc1: Electrification

**OR**

**Path 2. Plan for Decarbonization**
Create a plan detailing how decarbonization could be achieved through a 25-year period. The building owner or owner's representative shall attest that they have reviewed the decarbonization plan.

Plan requirements:
- The plan shall be a **narrative no more than two pages** in length
- The narrative shall describe the retrofits to be made, with the approximate timeline and cost of each of the retrofit measures
- Equipment and/or building materials that will be discarded as a result of the required retrofits should be described along with new equipment to be purchased
- Electrification readiness strategies incorporated into the initial design should be described along with a rough estimate of the **avoided cost, avoided disruption, and avoided materials waste** afforded by each readiness measure
- Core and Shell projects should incorporate strategies to support tenant build-out and address future retrofits after tenant build-out
- Common readiness strategies include: oversizing electrical panels and/or service or installing conduit for future loads, enhanced envelope, or heating distribution systems that can accommodate the lower temperatures of future heat pumps

### Data/Calculations Required
1. **Annual site energy estimates** by energy type - must be submitted to USGBC
2. **25-year BAU carbon projection** - generated by USGBC from submitted data
3. For BPS-subject projects: ordinance-specific BAU with caps overlay and cumulative fine/fee calculations
4. Decarbonization plan narrative (max 2 pages) OR EAc1 electrification points (4-5 pts)

### Documentation to Submit to USGBC
- Annual site energy use data by energy type
- Owner attestation of reviewing BAU carbon projections
- Owner attestation of reviewing decarbonization plan (Path 2) OR evidence of EAc1 points (Path 1)
- For BPS projects: ordinance-specific BAU with cap overlay and fine calculations

## EAp3: FUNDAMENTAL COMMISSIONING

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Credit ID** | EAp3 |
| **Type** | Prerequisite (Required) |
| **Applicability** | New Construction, Core and Shell |
| **Points** | N/A (Required) |
| **Impact Area** | Decarbonization |

### Intent
To improve energy performance and limit greenhouse gas emissions by verifying that systems are operating per the owner's project requirements.

### Achievement Pathways

Comply with **ANSI/ASHRAE/IES Standard 90.1 commissioning requirements** for building systems, controls, and the building envelope, with the following additional provisions:

- All projects shall provide **fundamental commissioning**. Section 4.2.5.2 exceptions shall not apply.
- The referenced version of Standard 90.1 with errata shall be:
  - **2019 or later** for projects registered before January 1, 2028
  - **2022 or later** for projects registered on or after January 1, 2028

#### CxP Requirements
By the end of the design development phase, the owner shall designate a **commissioning provider (CxP)** with experience completing commissioning on at least **two projects of equal or larger scope and complexity**.

In addition to ASHRAE 90.1 requirements, the CxP shall:

**Predesign or as early as possible:**
- Assist in the development of the OPR (Owner's Project Requirements)
- Review and update the OPR through design and construction
- OPR must include: HVAC, service water heating, power, lighting, other equipment (include on-site renewable energy), and envelope

**During design:**
- Review the BOD (Basis of Design) for compliance with the OPR
- Attend at least **one meeting focused on MEP** and **one focused on envelope** (may be separate or combined) to discuss review comments and commissioning

**During construction:**
- Review submittals and substitutions for design deviations that impact the OPR
- Attend milestone meetings at **50% and 100% completion**
- Perform a **sample review (minimum 10%)** of completed contractor documentation for QA/QC
- For envelope: include testing in commissioning documents and **witness a sample of tests** (not required for Core and Shell)

**Occupancy/operations phase:**
- Develop an **ongoing commissioning plan**

### Referenced Standards
- ANSI/ASHRAE/IES Standard 90.1-2019 or 2022 (commissioning requirements)

### Documentation Required
- OPR document (including HVAC, SWH, power, lighting, renewable energy, envelope)
- BOD document
- CxP qualifications (2+ projects of equal/larger scope)
- TAB reports
- Commissioning report
- Ongoing commissioning plan

## EAp4: ENERGY METERING AND REPORTING

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Credit ID** | EAp4 |
| **Type** | Prerequisite (Required) |
| **Applicability** | New Construction, Core and Shell |
| **Points** | N/A (Required) |
| **Impact Area** | Decarbonization |

### Intent
To support energy management practices and facilitate identification of ongoing opportunities for energy and greenhouse gas emissions savings by tracking and reporting building energy use and demand.

### Achievement Pathways

#### Energy Monitoring and Recording
- Install (or use existing) devices to monitor and record energy use per ANSI/ASHRAE/IES Standard 90.1
  - Version: **2019 or later** (registered before Jan 1, 2028) OR **2022 or later** (registered on/after Jan 1, 2028)
- Install (or use existing) devices to monitor and record energy use for the following (meeting same monitoring/reporting requirements as ASHRAE for electrical end uses):
  - **On-site renewable electricity generation**
- Major renovations and buildings eligible for exceptions to ASHRAE 90.1-2019 Section 10.4.6 or 90.1-2022 Section 10.4.7 must install measurement devices capable of monitoring:
  - **Whole-building energy use for each building energy source**
  - **Building peak electricity demand** (at least monthly)

#### Report Energy Data
Commit to **reporting the following data to USGBC at least annually** for **5 years** (or until building changes ownership/lessee):
- Monthly energy data for 12 consecutive months of total energy consumption for each energy source
- On-site renewable energy generation
- Peak electrical demand

**Exception for Core and Shell:** Future tenant utility services and meters that will be installed in tenant scope of work are excluded.

### Data Required for Submission to USGBC
- Monthly energy consumption data for 12 consecutive months
- Data by energy source (electricity, gas, steam, etc.)
- On-site renewable energy generation data
- Peak electrical demand data
- Annual commitment for 5 years post-occupancy

### Referenced Standards
- ANSI/ASHRAE/IES Standard 90.1-2019 or 2022 (Section 10.4.6/10.4.7)

<a name="eap5"></a>
## EAp5: FUNDAMENTAL REFRIGERANT MANAGEMENT

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Credit ID** | EAp5 |
| **Type** | Prerequisite (Required) |
| **Applicability** | New Construction, Core and Shell |
| **Points** | N/A (Required) |
| **Impact Area** | Decarbonization |

### Intent
To reduce greenhouse gas emissions from refrigerants by accelerating the phaseout of refrigerants with high global warming potential and by reducing refrigerant leakage.

### Achievement Pathways

#### Option 1. No Refrigerants
Do not use refrigerants in the project.

#### OR

#### Option 2. Refrigerants
Meet the following requirements:

1. **Complete refrigerant inventory**: Complete an inventory of refrigerant-containing equipment installed within the project scope of work and any existing equipment owned by the building owner. The inventory shall include:
   - Refrigerant type
   - GWP (Global Warming Potential)
   - Amounts of refrigerants contained in each
   - Total GWP of all refrigerants

2. **Do not use hydrochlorofluorocarbon (HCFC) refrigerants** in new equipment

3. **Evaluate available alternatives** during the design process for any refrigerants with **GWP > 700**

4. **Leak check and repair**: Prior to substantial completion:
   - Check both new and existing refrigerant-containing equipment for refrigerant leaks
   - Repair all identified leaks
   - For systems with field-assembled joints: perform leak check, vacuum check, and pressure check prior to charging with refrigerant

### Data/Calculations Required
- Complete refrigerant inventory (type, GWP, amounts, total GWP)
- Documentation of HCFC-free new equipment
- Alternative refrigerant evaluation for GWP > 700
- Leak check documentation
- Repair records

<a name="eac1"></a>
## EAc1: ELECTRIFICATION

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Credit ID** | EAc1 |
| **Type** | Credit |
| **Applicability** | New Construction (1-5 points), Core and Shell (1-4 points) |
| **Platinum Requirement** | 5 points (New Construction), 4 points (Core and Shell) |
| **Impact Area** | Decarbonization |

### Intent
To encourage building design that does not depend on burning fuel on-site, which leads to better indoor and outdoor air quality and to low-carbon operations as the grid decarbonizes.

---

### REQUIREMENTS: NEW CONSTRUCTION (1-5 points)

#### Option 1. No On-Site Combustion (5 points)
Design and operate the project from start-up with **no on-site combustion** except for emergency support systems.

- Combined weighted average equipment efficiency for space heating and service water heating (SWH) must be at least **1.8 COP**
- Equipment that may be **excluded** from COP determination:
  - Space-heating equipment in climate zones 0-2
  - Supplemental heating equipment designed only for operation at low temperatures
  - SWH equipment in nonresidential spaces complying with point-of-use water heater criteria in ASHRAE 90.1-2022, Section 11.5.2.3.3, W05, without exceptions

#### Option 2. No On-Site Combustion Except at Low Temperatures (1-4 points)
Pursue any combination of the following paths for maximum 4 points:

**Path 1. Space Heating (2 points)**
Design space heating to be capable of operating without on-site combustion except at low temperatures.
- Projects in climate zones 3 and above: weighted average space heating equipment efficiency of at least **1.8 COP**
- Excluded: Supplemental/auxiliary heating equipment designed only for low temperature operation

**Path 2. Service Water Heating (1 point)**
Design SWH systems to be capable of operating without on-site combustion except at low temperatures.
- Projects with total SWH capacity exceeding **34,000 Btu/hr (10 kW)**: weighted average SWH efficiency of at least **1.8 COP** OR domestic hot water solar fraction of at least **0.4**
- Excluded: Point-of-use water heaters per ASHRAE 90.1-2022 Section 11.5.2.3.3 W05; supplemental/auxiliary low-temp equipment

**Path 3. Cooking and Other Process Loads (1 point)**
Design cooking, laundry, process equipment, and on-site power generation (except emergency support systems) to operate without on-site combustion. Projects without these systems automatically earn this point.
- Excluded: Process heating equipment designed for operation at low temperatures

### Equipment Efficiency Determination
Determine weighted average COP using either:
- Equipment efficiencies at rated conditions: Use rating closest to **17 degF (-9 degC) OA db**, 32 degF (0 degC) entering liquid temperature, or 44 degF (6 degC) heating source leaving liquid temperature
- Annual average COP calculated with energy simulation

**District energy**: Must comply at district facility or see additional guidance
**Fuel cells**: Fuel cells using fossil fuel are ineligible
**Low temperatures**: OA db below 20 degF (-6.5 degC)

---

### REQUIREMENTS: CORE AND SHELL (1-4 points)

#### Option 1. Electrification (2-4 points)

**General rule for Paths 1 and 2:**
- Include all heating and SWH systems necessary to meet total building heating and SWH load in COP calculations
- Future heating or SWH systems must be included with a **COP of 1.0**
- Future equipment may be excluded when applicable building code or construction drawings confirm weighted average COP of at least 1.8 for future installed equipment

**Path 1. No On-Site Combustion (3-4 points)**
No on-site combustion except emergency support systems.
- Combined weighted average equipment efficiency for space heating and SWH:
  - **At least 1.8 COP = 4 points**
  - **At least 1.3 COP = 3 points**
- Excluded: Space heating in CZ 0-2; supplemental low-temp; SWH < 34,000 Btu/hr; point-of-use water heaters per ASHRAE 90.1-2022 W05

**Path 2. No On-Site Combustion Except at Low Temperatures (1-3 points)**

*Case 1. Space Heating (1-2 points)*
Weighted average space heating equipment efficiency:
- **At least 1.8 COP = 2 points**
- **At least 1.3 COP = 1 point**
- Excluded: Space heating in CZ 0-2; supplemental low-temp equipment

*Case 2. Service Water Heating (1 point)*
SWH without on-site combustion except at low temperatures. For total SWH capacity > 34,000 Btu/hr (10 kW):
- Weighted average SWH efficiency of at least **1.8 COP** OR solar fraction of at least **0.4**

**Path 3. No On-Site Combustion - Limited Scope (1-2 points)**
Do not install on-site combustion equipment except emergency support systems.
- Combined weighted average efficiency for space heating and SWH: at least **1.8 COP**

| Minimum Project Scope of Work | Points |
|---|---|
| One or more heating, SWH, or process heating systems | 1 |
| At least 30% of project's peak combined heating and SWH load | 2 |

#### Option 2. Electrification Readiness (1 point)
Provide building infrastructure ensuring capability of operating without on-site combustion except at low temperatures and of installing heating/SWH systems with weighted average COP of at least 1.8.

Required infrastructure:
- Dedicated physical space for future electric space heating, SWH, or process heating equipment (sufficient size for outdoor heat pump equipment)
- Chase ways with space for refrigerant lines, condensate drainage, or other required piping
- When electrical distribution is in project scope: junction box in same physical space as future electric equipment; dedicated electrical panel space for appropriately phased branch circuit
- For portions without ventilation air in scope: space/accommodations capable of supporting energy recovery ventilation for at least 50% of ventilation air

Include electrification readiness details in project plans and tenant guidelines.

### Calculations Required
- Weighted average COP calculations for space heating and SWH
- Climate zone determination
- Equipment capacity totals
- Solar fraction calculations (if applicable)
- For Core and Shell: future system COP projections (1.0 default or 1.8+ with documentation)

### Referenced Standards
- ASHRAE 90.1-2022, Section 11.5.2.3.3, W05 (point-of-use water heater criteria)

<a name="eac2"></a>
## EAc2: REDUCE PEAK THERMAL LOADS

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Credit ID** | EAc2 |
| **Type** | Credit |
| **Applicability** | New Construction (1-5 points), Core and Shell (1-5 points) |
| **Impact Area** | Decarbonization |

### Intent
To minimize demand on grid resources and improve the resilience of buildings.

### Achievement Pathways
Comply with any combination of Options 1-4 for maximum 5 points.

**Envelope requirement for all options:** Building envelope must meet ASHRAE 90.1, Section 5.5 (Prescriptive) or Section 5.6 (Trade-Off). Building envelope efficiency shall **not be traded off** with other building systems.

---

#### Option 1. Infiltration and Balanced Ventilation (1 point)

**Balanced Ventilation:**
Design ventilation and exhaust airflows within **10% of each other**. Include TAB report demonstrating balanced ventilation in commissioning scope. (Not applicable to Core and Shell)

**AND**

**Infiltration:**
Use air leakage test demonstrating measured air leakage less than or equal to:

| Conditioned Floor Area | Pressure Test | Max Air Leakage - New Construction | Max Air Leakage - Major Renovation |
|---|---|---|---|
| >= 5,000 sq. ft. (465 sq. m.) | 50 Pa | 0.13 cfm/sq. ft. (0.65 L/s/m2) | 0.18 cfm/sq. ft. (0.90 L/s/m2) |
| >= 5,000 sq. ft. (465 sq. m.) | 75 Pa | 0.20 cfm/sq. ft. (1.0 L/s/m2) | 0.27 cfm/sq. ft. (1.35 L/s/m2) |
| < 5,000 sq. ft. (465 sq. m.) | 50 Pa | 1 ACH | 1.5 ACH |
| < 5,000 sq. ft. (465 sq. m.) | 75 Pa | 1.35 ACH | 2 ACH |

Testing standards: ASTM E779, ANSI/RESNET/ICC 380, ASTM E3158, ASTM E1827, or equivalent
- For buildings > 5,000 sq. ft.: maximum air leakage per sq. ft. of envelope area (exterior walls, roofs, base floor/slab)
- For mixed new construction + renovation: use weighted average

**Residential (2 points):**
- Balanced ventilation per above
- Compartmentalize each dwelling unit
- Blower door test per ANSI/RESNET/ICC 380: max leakage 1.5x Table 1 thresholds
- Weighted average leakage for building complies with Table 1

---

#### Option 2. Ventilation Energy Recovery (1 point)
Each fan system supplying outdoor air must have energy/heat recovery with:
- Minimum **70% enthalpy recovery ratio** OR
- Minimum **75% sensible heat recovery ratio**

Provisions for bypass/control during moderate outside air conditions.

In aggregate, fan systems supplying less than 15% of project's total outdoor air can be excluded.

**Core and Shell only:** Fan systems must supply minimum 50% of ventilation air required per ASHRAE 62.1-2022 to qualify.

---

#### Option 3. Thermal Bridging (1 point)
Comply with prescriptive thermal bridging requirements of **ASHRAE 90.1-2022, Section 5.5.5(a)**, including all applicable requirements of Sections 5.5.5.1-5.5.5.5, **without applying exceptions** for projects in climate zones 0-3.

---

#### Option 4. Peak Thermal Load Reductions (1-3 points)

Requirements:
- Ventilation loads must be included in determination of peak coincident loads
- Measure building envelope air leakage using air leakage testing and use measured air leakage to calculate peak loads (meeting Option 1 rates is NOT required)
- Demonstrate balanced ventilation meeting Option 1 criteria

**Path 1. Peak Load Intensity (1-3 points)**
Limit sum of peak heating load + peak cooling load per treated floor area:

| Points | New Construction | Major Renovation |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | <= 16 Btu/h/sq. ft. (50 W/sq. m.) | <= 20 Btu/h/sq. ft. (63 W/sq. m.) |

### Intent
To reduce greenhouse gas emissions by accelerating the use of refrigerants with low global warming potential (GWP) and promoting better refrigerant management practices.

### Achievement Pathways
Comply with any combination of Options 1-3 for maximum 2 points.

---

#### Option 1. No Refrigerants or Low GWP (1-2 points)

**Path 1. No Refrigerants (1 point)**
Do not use refrigerant-containing equipment in the project.

**Core and Shell only:** Projects without future equipment necessary to meet heating/cooling load are ineligible.

**Path 2. Low GWP Refrigerants (1-2 points)**
Maximum total weighted average refrigerant GWP in all new equipment:
- **<= 80% of benchmark GWP = 1 point**
- **<= 50% of benchmark GWP = 2 points**

Projects that limit effective refrigerant GWP by reducing refrigerant charge per unit of capacity relative to comparable equipment may use adjusted benchmarks.

**Refrigerant GWP Benchmarks (100-year time horizon):**

| Equipment/Systems | GWP Benchmark |
|---|---|
| Heat pump service hot water heaters | 1,400 |
| HVAC | 1,400 |
| Data centers, computer room AC, IT equipment cooling | 700 |
| Process chiller equipment or ice rink refrigeration | 700 |
| All other process refrigeration for retail, industrial, cold storage | 300 |

---

#### Option 2. Limit Refrigerant Leakage (1 point)

**Core and Shell only:** Applicable only to projects with at least 20% of total estimated heating and cooling generation capacity in project scope.

Design, construct, and operate to minimize refrigerant leakage:

**DESIGN:**
- Refrigerant-using equipment shall be **self-contained, with no field-installed piping**:
  - For equipment with refrigerants > 700 GWP, AND
  - For at least 80% of the total GWP of refrigerants used
- Specify automatic leak detection system in fully enclosed spaces with equipment having overall refrigerant charge exceeding **100 tCO2e**

**INSTALLATION:**
- Field-installed refrigerant piping shall use **brazed or press-type fittings**

**OPERATION:**
- Refrigerant maintenance plan with designated responsible oversight party
- Plan includes:
  - Updating refrigerant inventory
  - Tracking/recording refrigerant charge and leakage rates for all equipment
  - Ensuring installation, maintenance, removal by appropriately certified refrigeration personnel (including tenant spaces)
  - Annual audit and calibration of automatic leak detection systems
  - For equipment without automatic leak detection: pressure loss/leak checks at intervals based on tCO2e:
    - Every 24 months for 50 tCO2e or less
    - Every 12 months for 50-500 tCO2e
    - Every 3 months for > 500 tCO2e
  - Identifying maximum time frame for repairing leaks
  - Doubling check frequency if total annual refrigerant recharge/leakage exceeds 1%

#### Option 3. GreenChill Certification for Food Retailers (1-2 points)
Available to projects where food retailing constitutes **more than 20%** of project's gross area.

- **GreenChill Silver certification = 1 point**
- **GreenChill Gold or Platinum certification = 2 points**

For international projects: comply with relevant GreenChill requirements for certification level.

**For all options:**
**District Energy:** Projects with district energy must comply at district facility or see additional guidance.

### Calculations Required
- Weighted average refrigerant GWP calculations
- Comparison to benchmark GWP (80% or 50%)
- Refrigerant charge calculations (tCO2e)
- Leak rate tracking and calculations
- Percentage of food retailing gross area (for Option 3)

### Referenced Standards/Programs
- EPA GreenChill Certification program
- GWP values based on 100-year time horizon relative to CO2

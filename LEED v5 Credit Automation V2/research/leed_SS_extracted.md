## <a name="ssp1"></a>SSp1 — MINIMIZED SITE DISTURBANCE (Prerequisite)

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Credit ID** | SSp1 |
| **Name** | Minimized Site Disturbance |
| **Type** | Prerequisite (Required) |
| **New Construction** | Required |
| **Core and Shell** | Required |
| **Points** | N/A |
| **Impact Area Alignment** | Ecological Conservation and Restoration |

### INTENT
To limit site disturbance from construction activities and preserve existing native vegetation, healthy soils, and wildlife habitats.

### ACHIEVEMENT PATHWAYS
**New Construction and Core and Shell**

| Pathway | Requirement |
|---------|-------------|
| Erosion and Sedimentation Control Plan | **AND** |
| Site Assessment | **AND** |

---

### REQUIREMENT 1: Erosion and Sedimentation Control Plan

Create and implement an **erosion and sedimentation control plan** for all construction activities associated with the project. The plan must conform to the erosion and sedimentation requirements of:
- **U.S. EPA's 2022 Construction General Permit (CGP)**; OR
- **EU Taxonomy: DNSH, Pollution Prevention, Item 4 Noise and Dust**; OR
- **Local equivalent**

> **Note:** Projects must apply the CGP regardless of size.

The erosion and sedimentation control plan **must also include** implementation of the following measures:

| Measure | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Construction-exclusion zones | Establishment of zones demarcated by physical barriers and stormwater controls to protect any identified critical habitat for threatened or endangered species from discharges and discharge-related activities |
| Site inspections | At least once every 7 calendar days, OR once every 14 calendar days **and** within 24 hours of a storm event producing 0.25 inches (6 mm) or more of rain within a 24-hour period |
| Dewatering inspections | Once per day on which discharge of dewatering occurs |
| Corrective actions | Immediate corrective actions to repair or replace controls when failing |

---

### REQUIREMENT 2: Site Assessment

Collect information about the site in a **preconstruction survey or assessment** that informs design of the site to address the following items, as applicable:

| Site Feature | Requirement |
|-------------|-------------|
| **Special-status vegetation** | Conserve **100%** of special-status vegetation located on-site as defined by local, state, or federal entities |
| **Healthy habitat** | Identify healthy plant communities; implement strategies to minimize damage during construction; establish exclusion zones demarcated by physical barriers |
| **Invasive vegetation** | Indicate locations of existing invasive vegetation species; address removal and control before and during construction; include only native and adapted vegetation not currently listed as invasive |

> The survey/assessment must demonstrate the relationships between the site features and how these features influenced the project design.

---

### DOCUMENTATION REQUIRED
- [ ] Erosion and Sedimentation Control Plan (conforming to EPA 2022 CGP, EU Taxonomy, or local equivalent)
- [ ] Construction-exclusion zone documentation (physical barrier plans, stormwater controls)
- [ ] Site inspection schedule and records
- [ ] Preconstruction survey or assessment report
- [ ] Documentation of special-status vegetation conservation (100%)
- [ ] Healthy plant community identification and exclusion zone plans
- [ ] Invasive vegetation inventory and removal/control plan
- [ ] Native/adapted vegetation specification

---

### CALCULATIONS REQUIRED
None specified (planning and documentation based).

### DATA TABLES / THRESHOLDS

| Parameter | Threshold |
|-----------|-----------|
| Storm event triggering post-storm inspection | 0.25 inches (6 mm) within 24 hours |
| Special-status vegetation conservation | 100% |
| Site inspection frequency | Every 7 days (or every 14 days + post-storm) |
| Dewatering inspection frequency | Daily |

## <a name="ssc1"></a>SSc1 — BIODIVERSE HABITAT

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Credit ID** | SSc1 |
| **Name** | Biodiverse Habitat |
| **Type** | Credit |
| **New Construction** | 1–2 points |
| **Core and Shell** | 1–2 points |
| **Impact Area Alignment** | Ecological Conservation and Restoration |

### INTENT
To conserve existing natural areas, enhance biodiversity, restore damaged areas, and provide thriving habitats for local wildlife.

---

### ACHIEVEMENT PATHWAYS

| Option | Path | Points (NC) | Points (C&S) |
|--------|------|-------------|--------------|
| **Option 1. Preserve and Restore Habitat** | Path 1. Greenfield Sites | 1 | 1 |
| **Option 1. Preserve and Restore Habitat** | Path 2. Previously Disturbed Sites | 1–2 | 1–2 |
| **Option 2. Bird-Friendly Glass** | — | 1 | 1 |

Projects may pursue **Option 1 AND/OR Option 2**.

---

### OPTION 1, PATH 1: Greenfield Sites (1 point)

Preserve **40% of the greenfield area** on the site by protecting these areas from all development and construction activity.

---

### OPTION 1, PATH 2: Previously Disturbed Sites (1–2 points)

Meet Path 1 requirements (if greenfield areas exist) **AND** restore previously disturbed areas.

> **Exemption:** Dedicated athletic fields solely for athletic use are exempt from counting toward total site area. These areas may not count toward protected greenfield or restored habitat areas.

**Points Table — Percentage of Area Restored**

| Restored Area | Zero Lot Line | Points |
|---------------|---------------|--------|
| 20% of previously disturbed area | 10% | 1 |
| 40% of previously disturbed area | 20% | 2 |

---

#### Soil Restoration Requirements

Restore all on-site soils disturbed by previous development and soils disturbed by current construction activities that will serve as final vegetated areas. 

**Import restrictions:** Imported soils must be reused in a way comparable to their original function and **may NOT include**:
- Soils defined regionally by NRCS web soil survey (or local equivalent) as prime farmland, unique farmland, or farmland of statewide/local importance
- Soils from other greenfield sites
- Sphagnum peat moss or organic amendments containing sphagnum peat

> **Exemption:** Engineered growing medium for vegetated roofs are exempt from soil restoration requirements.

---

#### Vegetation Restoration Requirements

Plant native and adapted vegetation that is **not currently listed as invasive**, including:

| Requirement | Detail |
|-------------|--------|
| Species diversity | At least **10 species** native or adapted to the project's **EPA Level III ecoregion** (or local equivalent) |
| Plant categories | Minimum of **2 categories** from: trees, shrubs, ground cover |
| Pollinator habitat | At least **110 sq ft (10 sq m)** of native flowering plants for local pollinators; in groupings of at least 10 sq ft (1 sq m) each |
| Signage | Pollinator habitat area designated with signage |

> Zero lot line projects are exempt from the 2-category requirement.

---

### OPTION 2: Bird-Friendly Glass (1 point)

Glass used below specified heights must have a **maximum threat factor of 30**, per the American Bird Conservancy's Threat Factor Database.

**Applicability:**

| Location | Height Threshold |
|----------|-----------------|
| From grade | Up to **50 feet (15 m)** measured at all points |
| Green roofs | Up to **20 feet (6 m)** measured from finished grade |
| Guardrails and windshields | **At any distance** from grade or roof |

This applies to **all glass, including spandrel glass**.

---

### DOCUMENTATION REQUIRED
- [ ] Site plan showing greenfield areas (40% protected)
- [ ] Site plan showing previously disturbed areas with restoration calculations
- [ ] Soil restoration plan and documentation of imported soil sources
- [ ] Vegetation species list (minimum 10 native/adapted species)
- [ ] Plant category documentation (trees, shrubs, ground cover — 2+ categories)
- [ ] Pollinator habitat plan with square footage and signage details
- [ ] Bird-friendly glass threat factor documentation (ABC Threat Factor Database)
- [ ] Glass schedule showing locations below threshold heights

---

### CALCULATIONS REQUIRED

| Calculation | Method |
|-------------|--------|
| Greenfield preservation percentage | (Protected greenfield area / Total greenfield area) x 100; must be >= 40% |
| Previously disturbed area restored | (Restored disturbed area / Total previously disturbed area) x 100 |
| Pollinator habitat area | Sum of all native flowering plant groupings; must be >= 110 sq ft (10 sq m) in groupings of >= 10 sq ft (1 sq m) |
| Bird-friendly glass compliance | Verify all applicable glass has threat factor <= 30 per ABC database |

---

### DATA TABLES / THRESHOLDS

| Parameter | Threshold |
|-----------|-----------|
| Greenfield preservation | 40% |
| Restored area — Standard (1 pt) | 20% of previously disturbed area |
| Restored area — Standard (2 pts) | 40% of previously disturbed area |
| Restored area — Zero lot line (1 pt) | 10% |
| Restored area — Zero lot line (2 pts) | 20% |
| Native/adapted species required | 10 minimum |
| Plant categories required | 2 of 3 (trees, shrubs, ground cover) |
| Pollinator habitat | 110 sq ft (10 sq m), groupings of 10 sq ft (1 sq m) |
| Bird-friendly glass threat factor | Max 30 |
| Bird-friendly glass height — from grade | Up to 50 ft (15 m) |
| Bird-friendly glass height — green roof | Up to 20 ft (6 m) |

## <a name="ssc2"></a>SSc2 — ACCESSIBLE OUTDOOR SPACE

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Credit ID** | SSc2 |
| **Name** | Accessible Outdoor Space |
| **Type** | Credit |
| **New Construction** | 1 point |
| **Core and Shell** | 1 point |
| **Points** | 1 |
| **Impact Area Alignment** | Quality of Life |

### INTENT
To create outdoor open space that encourages interaction with the environment, social and physical activities, and passive recreation, and to incorporate elements that celebrate the community served.

---

### ACHIEVEMENT PATHWAYS

| Requirement | Description | Points |
|-------------|-------------|--------|
| Sufficient Outdoor Space Area | AND | 1 |
| Urban Outdoor Space | AND | — |
| Community Outdoor Space | | — |

All three requirements must be met for 1 point.

---

### REQUIREMENT 1: Sufficient Outdoor Space Area

- Provide **barrier-free and physically accessible** outdoor space for people with limited mobility
- Space must be **>= 30% of total site area** (including building footprint)
- At least **25% of the required outdoor space** must be **vegetated** and planted with **two or more types of vegetation** OR have an **overhead vegetative canopy**

---

### REQUIREMENT 2: Urban Outdoor Space

Include **one or more** of the following elements:

| Element | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Biophilic space** | Area meeting SSc1 vegetation restoration requirements + elements of human interaction (observation platforms or paths) |
| **Garden** | Space dedicated to community gardens or urban food production |
| **Recreational area** | Recreation-oriented paving/landscape encouraging physical activity (courts, fields, track, play space, swimming pools) |
| **Social area** | Pedestrian-oriented paving/landscape for outdoor social activities; includes **seating for 5% of occupants** |

---

### REQUIREMENT 3: Community Outdoor Space

Include **one or more** of the following elements:

| Element | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Community** | Publicly accessible during daylight hours; open to all community members |
| **Cultural** | At least **two art installations or sculptures by local artists** |
| **Acoustics** | Elements providing positive soundscapes if located within **0.24 miles (400 m)** of a significant noise source (roadway, airport, rail line, etc.) |

---

### DOCUMENTATION REQUIRED
- [ ] Site plan showing outdoor space area calculation (>= 30% of total site)
- [ ] Accessibility plan (barrier-free design)
- [ ] Vegetation documentation for 25% of outdoor space (species, canopy coverage)
- [ ] Urban outdoor space element documentation (one or more selected)
- [ ] Community outdoor space element documentation (one or more selected)
- [ ] Occupancy count for seating calculation (if social area selected)
- [ ] Artist information for art installations (if cultural element selected)
- [ ] Noise source assessment and acoustic element documentation (if applicable)

---

### CALCULATIONS REQUIRED

| Calculation | Formula |
|-------------|---------|
| Outdoor space area | Total outdoor space / Total site area >= 30% |
| Vegetated space | Vegetated area / Required outdoor space >= 25% |
| Seating capacity (if social area) | Number of seats / Total occupants >= 5% |

---

### DATA TABLES / THRESHOLDS

| Parameter | Threshold |
|-----------|-----------|
| Outdoor space as % of total site | >= 30% |
| Vegetated portion of outdoor space | >= 25% |
| Vegetation types | 2 or more |
| Seating (social area) | 5% of occupants |
| Art installations (cultural element) | 2 by local artists |
| Noise source proximity for acoustic requirement | 0.24 miles (400 m) |

---

## <a name="ssc3"></a>SSc3 — RAINWATER MANAGEMENT

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Credit ID** | SSc3 |
| **Name** | Rainwater Management |
| **Type** | Credit |
| **New Construction** | 1–3 points |
| **Core and Shell** | 1–3 points |
| **Impact Area Alignment** | Ecological Conservation and Restoration |

### INTENT
To reduce runoff volume and improve water quality by replicating the natural hydrology and water balance of the site, based on historical conditions and undeveloped ecosystems in the region, to avoid contributing to flooding downstream in frontline communities.

---

### ACHIEVEMENT PATHWAYS

| Option | Description | Points |
|--------|-------------|--------|
| **Option 1** | Percentile of Rainfall Events | 1–3 |
| **Option 2** | Natural Land Cover Conditions | 3 |

Choose one option.

---

### OPTION 1: Percentile of Rainfall Events (1–3 points)

In a manner replicating natural site hydrology processes, **retain the runoff** from the associated percentile of regional or local rainfall events on site. The percentile event volume must be retained (infiltrated, evapotranspired, or collected and reused) using **LID and GI practices**.

**Eligible strategies:** Structural or nonstructural GI and LID strategies.

**Additional point for water reuse:** Projects that collect and reuse a portion of the chosen percentile event volume for building/grounds end uses earn **1 additional point**. Eligible end uses: irrigation, flush fixtures, makeup water systems (cooling towers, boilers), other process water demands.

> Collecting and reusing rainwater can also contribute to Water Efficiency credit category points.

**Points Table — Percentile of Rainfall Events**

| All Projects | Zero Lot Line Projects | Points | Total Points for Water Reuse |
|--------------|------------------------|--------|------------------------------|
| 80th percentile | 70th percentile | 1 | 2 |
| 85th percentile | 75th percentile | 2 | 3 |
| 90th percentile | 80th percentile | 3 | — |

---

### OPTION 2: Natural Land Cover Conditions (3 points)

Calculate the difference between:
- Projected runoff volume under **proposed design conditions**
- Runoff volume under **natural land cover conditions** (prior to any disturbance)

**Retain** (infiltrate, evapotranspire, or collect and reuse) the **increase in runoff volume** on site using LID and GI practices.

---

### SPECIAL PROVISION: Zero Lot Line Projects

Project teams may combine **on-site and off-site strategies** to retain runoff from the associated percentile regional or local rainfall event per Table 1. Must engage with local/regional authorities to coordinate off-site strategies (e.g., community-wide rainwater management programs).

---

### DOCUMENTATION REQUIRED
- [ ] Rainwater management plan
- [ ] Regional/local rainfall event data (percentile calculations)
- [ ] Hydrology model showing natural vs. proposed runoff volumes
- [ ] LID/GI strategy documentation
- [ ] Water reuse system documentation (if pursuing additional point)
- [ ] End use calculations for rainwater reuse
- [ ] Zero lot line projects: documentation of off-site coordination with authorities

---

### CALCULATIONS REQUIRED

| Calculation | Method |
|-------------|--------|
| Runoff volume for target percentile event | Use regional/local rainfall data; apply appropriate hydrology model (e.g., NRCS Curve Number, SWMM) |
| Retained runoff volume | Sum of infiltration + evapotranspiration + collection/reuse volumes |
| Natural land cover runoff (Option 2) | Model runoff under pre-development, natural land cover conditions |
| Proposed runoff (Option 2) | Model runoff under proposed developed conditions |
| Volume to retain (Option 2) | Proposed runoff - Natural land cover runoff |

---

### DATA TABLES / THRESHOLDS

**Table 1: Points for Percentile of Rainfall Events Retained**

| Project Type | 1 Point | 2 Points | 3 Points |
|-------------|---------|----------|----------|
| **All Projects** | 80th percentile | 85th percentile | 90th percentile |
| **Zero Lot Line** | 70th percentile | 75th percentile | 80th percentile |

| Parameter | Threshold |
|-----------|-----------|
| Additional point for water reuse | Collect and reuse for eligible end uses |
| Eligible end uses (water reuse) | Irrigation, flush fixtures, makeup water (cooling towers/boilers), other process water |
| LID/GI practices | Structural or nonstructural |

---

## <a name="ssc4"></a>SSc4 — ENHANCED RESILIENT SITE DESIGN

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Credit ID** | SSc4 |
| **Name** | Enhanced Resilient Site Design |
| **Type** | Credit |
| **New Construction** | 2 points |
| **Core and Shell** | 2 points |
| **Impact Area Alignment** | Quality of Life |

### INTENT
Reduce the risk of catastrophic impacts from natural and climate events on site and in adjacent landscapes by designing, building, and maintaining sites to be more resilient to observed, projected, and future climate and natural hazards.

---

### ACHIEVEMENT PATHWAYS

| Pathway | Points |
|---------|--------|
| Integrate Requirements for Two High-Priority Hazards | 2 |

Design and construct the site and site structures to meet best practices for **at least two** of the highest priority hazards identified in **IPp1: Climate Resilience Assessment**.

---

### HAZARD-SPECIFIC REQUIREMENTS

#### DROUGHT

| Strategy | Requirement |
|----------|-------------|
| Water efficiency | Comply with WEc2: Enhanced Water Efficiency |
| Plantings | Specify native and/or drought-tolerant adapted/appropriate plantings |
| Water features | Water makeup for any created water features must comply with SITES C3.4 or local equivalent |

**Core and Shell only:** Water makeup for any created water features must not exceed 5,000 gallons (18,927 liters) of potable water per year, **OR** 75% of annual water makeup must come from alternative water sources.

---

#### EXTREME HEAT

Integrate **two or more** of the following elements:

| Strategy | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| Shaded external spaces | Provide shaded spaces adjacent to buildings for use during extreme heat events |
| Evaporative cooling | Provide fountains, misters, water features, etc. |
| Building orientation | Orient buildings and massing to self-shade in summer/extreme heat |
| Cooling stations | Provide outdoor cooling stations with emergency backup power |
| Proximity to emergency cooling | Demonstrate proximity to emergency cooling station within 0.25 miles (400 m) |
| Paving materials | Use paving with initial solar reflectance (SR) >= 0.33 |
| Open-grid pavement | Use open-grid pavement system (at least 50% unbound) |

---

#### FLOODING

Integrate **two or more** strategies per **ASCE 24 and FEMA 543** (or local equivalent):

**Critical utilities:**
- Locate critical utilities in new construction above DFE + freeboard
- Retrofits: locate inside protective, floodproofed enclosures
- Design potable water systems to resist flood damage and infiltration
- Elevate on-site wellheads above landscape
- Design sewage systems to avoid backup
- Anchor plumbing conduits, water supply, gas lines, electric cables extending below DFE
- Anchor rainwater storage tanks to resist flood forces

**Additional strategies:**
- Use flood-resistant structural materials, finish materials, and connectors below DFE
- Certify project under qualifying flood-resilient design standard(s)

---

#### HAIL
Design and construct per **FORTIFIED Commercial High Wind and Hail Specific Design Requirements for Hail** (or local equivalent).

---

#### HURRICANES AND HIGH WINDS

| Condition | Standard |
|-----------|----------|
| Hurricane-prone areas | FORTIFIED Commercial Wind standards (or local equivalent) |
| High-wind areas | ASCE/SEI 7-10 wind design measures in specified FEMA zones (or local equivalent) |

**Additional requirements:**
- Install backup power systems in hurricane-prone regions
- Install electrical connections with transfer switch or docking station (storm switch) for backup power
- Create windbreaks using landscape, vegetation, and locally appropriate natural systems

---

#### SEA LEVEL RISE
Design to accommodate flooding based on sea level rise and storm surge projections for the project's design service life. **Plus meet two or more of:**

| Strategy | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| Elevated foundations | Minimize projected flood damage |
| Water-resistant materials | Materials resistant to projected water damage |
| Sealants | Prevent projected water infiltration |
| Flood barriers | Block projected floodwater |
| Green infrastructure | GI solutions for projected storm surge and runoff |
| Backup power | Maintain critical functions during projected flooding |
| Drainage | Integrated drainage for projected excess flooding |
| Community engagement | Planning, partnerships, workshops for vulnerable populations |
| Retrofitting | Enhance existing structures' resilience to future flood risks |

---

#### TSUNAMIS
Mitigate impact through site planning per **Designing for Tsunamis** (U.S. National Tsunami Hazard Mitigation Program) or local equivalent. **Plus incorporate:**
- Tsunami danger area and evacuation route signage
- Public-Alert-certified NOAA Weather Radio receivers in critical facilities/public venues (or local equivalent)

---

#### WILDFIRES
Follow wildfire management practices per **National Wildfire Coordinating Group Standards for Mitigation 2023** (or local equivalent), including:
- Wildland-urban interface design
- Vegetation management
- Debris disposal
- Fire safety for equipment
- Compliance with **SITES v2 credit 4.11: Reduce the risk of catastrophic wildfire**
- Fuel reduction using **Safer from the Start zone concept** (Appendix E)

---

#### WINTER STORMS
Meet **two or more** of:

| Strategy | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| Ingress/egress | Adequate for vehicles and snow removal equipment |
| Snow removal plan | Including compatible road materials, areas for accumulated snow, roof snow removal |
| Safe walking surfaces | Consider heated sidewalks with renewable energy |
| Snow-load planting | Native/adapted planting with capacity for heavy snow loads |

---

### DOCUMENTATION REQUIRED
- [ ] IPp1 Climate Resilience Assessment (reference document)
- [ ] Identification of two highest-priority hazards addressed
- [ ] Hazard-specific design strategy documentation
- [ ] Compliance documentation per referenced standards (ASCE 24, FEMA 543, FORTIFIED, etc.)
- [ ] Site plans showing implemented resilient strategies
- [ ] Utility protection plans (for flooding)
- [ ] Core and Shell only: water feature potable water use calculations
- [ ] Tsunami signage and communication equipment documentation (if applicable)
- [ ] Wildfire fuel reduction plan (if applicable)
- [ ] Snow removal plan (if applicable)

---

### CALCULATIONS REQUIRED

| Calculation | Method |
|-------------|--------|
| Water feature potable use (Core and Shell) | Annual potable water <= 5,000 gal OR alternative sources >= 75% |
| Extreme heat strategies | Count implemented strategies (minimum 2) |
| Flood strategies | Count implemented strategies (minimum 2) from ASCE 24/FEMA 543 |
| Sea level rise strategies | Count implemented strategies (minimum 2) |
| Winter storm strategies | Count implemented strategies (minimum 2) |

---

### DATA TABLES / THRESHOLDS

| Hazard | Requirement |
|--------|-------------|
| Drought | WEc2 compliance + native/drought-tolerant plantings + SITES C3.4 water features |
| Drought — C&S water limit | 5,000 gal/year potable max OR 75% alternative |
| Extreme heat | 2+ strategies from 7-item list |
| Flooding | 2+ strategies per ASCE 24/FEMA 543 |
| Hail | FORTIFIED Commercial Hail |
| Hurricanes | FORTIFIED Commercial Wind or ASCE/SEI 7-10 |
| Sea level rise | 2+ strategies from 9-item list |
| Tsunamis | Designing for Tsunamis + 2 signage/communication items |
| Wildfires | NWCG Standards 2023 + SITES v2 4.11 + Safer from the Start |
| Winter storms | 2+ strategies from 4-item list |
| Number of hazards required | 2 from IPp1 priority list |

---

## <a name="ssc5"></a>SSc5 — HEAT ISLAND REDUCTION

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Credit ID** | SSc5 |
| **Name** | Heat Island Reduction |
| **Type** | Credit |
| **New Construction** | 1–2 points |
| **Core and Shell** | 1–2 points |
| **Impact Area Alignment** | Decarbonization |

### INTENT
To mitigate disparate impacts on microclimates and habitats caused by heat islands and extreme heat events.

---

### ACHIEVEMENT PATHWAYS

| Option | Description | Points |
|--------|-------------|--------|
| **Option 1** | Nonroof and Roof | 1 |
| **Option 2** | Parking Under Cover | 1 |
| **Option 3** | Tree Equity | 1 |

Projects may choose **one or more options**. Maximum points: 2 (New Construction) / 2 (Core and Shell).

---

### OPTION 1: Nonroof and Roof (1 point)

Meet criteria using a **weighted average approach**:

**Equation 1: Weighted Nonroof and Roof Calculation**

```
(Area of nonroof measures / 0.5) + (Area of high-reflectance roof / 0.75) + (Area of vegetative roof / 0.75) >= Total paving area + Total roof area
```

Use any combination of nonroof, high-reflectance roof, and vegetative roof strategies so that the weighted sum >= sum of total pavement and roof areas. Each surface counted once only.

---

#### Nonroof Measures (eligible strategies)

| Strategy | Requirement |
|----------|-------------|
| **Shade over pavement** | Measured in plain view at noon; existing or new plants assuming 10-year canopy width, or vegetated structures; must be in place at occupancy permit |
| **Energy generation systems** | Solar thermal collectors, PVs, wind turbines covering structure |
| **Architectural devices/structures** | If roof: aged SR >= 0.28 per ANSI/CRRC S100; If not roof: initial SR >= 0.33 per ANSI/CRRC S100 |
| **Paving materials** | Initial SR value >= 0.33 |
| **Open-grid pavement** | At least 50% unbound |

---

#### High-Reflectance Roof

Use roofing materials with aged SRI >= values in Table 1. If aged SRI unavailable, use initial SRI >= values.

**Table 1: Minimum SRI Value, By Roof Slope**

| Roof Type | Slope | Initial SRI | Aged SRI |
|-----------|-------|-------------|----------|
| Low-sloped | <= 2:12 | 82 | 64 |
| Steep-sloped | > 2:12 | 39 | 32 |

> Functional roof spaces (helipads, recreation courts, pools) may meet nonroof measure requirements. Applicable roof area excludes mechanical equipment, solar panels, skylights, and appurtenances.

---

#### Vegetated Roof

Install vegetated roof using **native or adapted plant species**.

---

### OPTION 2: Parking Under Cover (1 point)

Place **100% of parking spaces under cover**. Covering roof must meet **at least one** of:

| Cover Type | Requirement |
|------------|-------------|
| High-reflectance roof | Aged SRI >= 32 (or initial SRI >= 39 if aged unavailable) |
| Vegetated roof | Native or adapted plants |
| Energy generation | Solar thermal, PV, wind turbines |

**Scope:** All existing and new off-street parking spaces subsidized, leased, or owned by the project, including parking outside project boundary but used by the project. On-street parking in public rights-of-way is excluded.

---

### OPTION 3: Tree Equity (1 point)

**U.S. projects:** Evaluate **American Forests Tree Equity Score** for the site location. In areas ranked "high priority" or "highest priority," use results to inform increase in on-site canopy cover from existing condition.

**International projects:** Refer to IPp2: Human Impact Assessment; evaluate tree cover on site and in surrounding community using local tree census or site assessment. Analyze local community composition for underserved/disadvantaged populations with lower tree canopy. Increase on-site canopy cover to provide shade to those areas.

> Projects with no exterior work are exempt.

---

### DOCUMENTATION REQUIRED
- [ ] Site plan with all nonroof (paving) areas quantified
- [ ] Roof plan with roof types and areas
- [ ] Weighted calculation showing compliance
- [ ] Product data for paving materials (SR values per ANSI/CRRC S100)
- [ ] Product data for roofing materials (SRI values)
- [ ] Vegetated roof design with native/adapted plant list
- [ ] Shade plan showing 10-year canopy widths
- [ ] Parking plan showing 100% covered (Option 2)
- [ ] Cover compliance documentation (SRI or vegetated or energy generation)
- [ ] American Forests Tree Equity Score report (U.S. projects, Option 3)
- [ ] Tree canopy analysis (international projects, Option 3)

---

### CALCULATIONS REQUIRED

**Equation 1: Weighted Nonroof and Roof**

```
[(Area_nonroof / 0.5) + (Area_high_reflectance_roof / 0.75) + (Area_vegetative_roof / 0.75)] >= Total_paving_area + Total_roof_area
```

| Surface Type | Weighting Factor | Minimum Value |
|--------------|-----------------|---------------|
| Nonroof (shade, SR>=0.33, open-grid, etc.) | 0.5 | Initial SR >= 0.33 |
| High-reflectance roof | 0.75 | Aged SRI: 64 (low-slope) / 32 (steep-slope) |
| Vegetative roof | 0.75 | Native/adapted plants |

| Parking Calculation (Option 2) | Method |
|-------------------------------|--------|
| Covered parking % | (Spaces covered / Total spaces) x 100 = 100% |

### INTENT
To increase night sky access, improve nighttime visibility, and reduce the consequences of development for wildlife and people.
